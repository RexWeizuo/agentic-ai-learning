# 惊吓与威胁

> **类型**: 近义

> **学习提示**: 表示使人害怕或受到威胁的词。startle (使惊吓), stun (使震惊/打昏), threat/threaten (威胁)。

**4 词**

---

## startle

- **音标**: 'stɑ:tl
- **释义**: n. 惊愕, 惊恐\nvt. 吃惊, 使惊愕\nvi. 惊起
- **英文释义**: to cause a sudden feeling of surprise or fear; to make someone jump or react quickly due to shock
- **词根词缀**: start(开始)+le(反复)→使吃惊
- **例句**:
- The sudden thunderclap startled the sleeping child.
- Her candid remarks startled even the most seasoned diplomats in the room.
- **考频**: 7
## stun

- **音标**: stʌn
- **释义**: vt. 使昏迷, 使震惊, 打昏\nn. 昏迷, 猛击
- **英文释义**: to make someone unconscious or extremely shocked, often suddenly
- **词根词缀**: stun(使震惊)→使震惊
- **例句**:
- The sudden news of her resignation stunned the entire board.
- He was stunned by a blow to the head and lost consciousness for several minutes.
- **考频**: 3
## threat

- **音标**: θret
- **释义**: n. 恐吓, 恶兆, 威胁\n[经] 威胁
- **英文释义**: Something that may cause harm, damage, or danger; a statement of intent to punish or harm someone if certain conditions are not met.
- **词根词缀**: threat(威胁)→威胁
- **例句**:
- The rise in cyberattacks poses a serious threat to national security.
- Her resignation would be a significant threat to the stability of the management team.
- **考频**: 148
## threaten

- **音标**: 'θretn
- **释义**: vt. 恐吓, 威胁, 预示...的凶兆\nvi. 威胁, 恫吓, 可能来临
- **英文释义**: to indicate that something bad may happen; to warn someone that you will harm them or cause them problems if they do not do what you want
- **词根词缀**: threat(威胁)+en(使)→使威胁→恐吓
- **例句**:
- The sudden drop in stock prices threatens the stability of the global financial system.
- She threatened to resign unless her proposal was approved by the board.
- **考频**: 116
