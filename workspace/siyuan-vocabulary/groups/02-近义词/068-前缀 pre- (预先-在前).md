# 前缀 pre- (预先/在前)

> **类型**: 词缀

> **学习提示**: pre- 表示“在...之前、预先”。如 precaution(预防), predict(预测), preface(前言)。

**10 词**

---

## precaution

- **音标**: pri'kɒ:ʃәn
- **释义**: n. 预防, 留心, 警戒\nvt. 预先警告
- **英文释义**: a measure taken in advance to prevent something dangerous, unpleasant, or undesirable from happening
- **词根词缀**: pre(前)+caution(小心)→事先小心→预防
- **例句**:
- The government has taken several precautionary measures to curb the spread of the virus.
- Before conducting the experiment, researchers observed all necessary safety precautions to avoid contamination.
- **考频**: 20
## preclude

- **音标**: pri'klu:d
- **释义**: vt. 预先排除, 预防, 阻止, 妨碍\n[法] 预防, 排除, 消除
- **英文释义**: to prevent something from happening or someone from doing something, often by prior action or existing conditions
- **词根词缀**: pre(前)+clude(关闭)→提前关闭→排除
- **例句**:
- The lack of funding precluded the research team from conducting large-scale clinical trials.
- Her contractual obligations preclude her from accepting any competing academic positions for the next two years.
- **考频**: 4
## predict

- **音标**: pri'dikt
- **释义**: v. 预知, 预言, 预报
- **英文释义**: to say that something will happen before it does, based on information or evidence
- **词根词缀**: pre(前)+dict(说)→提前说→预言
- **例句**:
- Economists predict that inflation will rise sharply next quarter.
- The weather forecast predicts heavy rain and thunderstorms across the region tomorrow.
- **考频**: 181
## preface

- **音标**: 'prefis
- **释义**: n. 序, 绪言, 前言\nvi. 作序, 写前言\nvt. 给...作序, 以...开始
- **英文释义**: A short introduction to a book, often written by the author or another person, explaining its purpose, scope, or background.
- **词根词缀**: pre(前)+face(脸)→前面的脸→前言
- **例句**:
- The author prefaces the novel with a personal reflection on memory and loss.
- In her preface, the scholar acknowledges the limitations of the study while outlining its theoretical framework.
- **考频**: 6
## prejudice

- **音标**: 'predʒudis

- **英文释义**: an unfair and unreasonable opinion or feeling, especially when formed without enough thought or knowledge
- **词根词缀**: pre(前)+jud(判断)+ice(名词)→预先判断→偏见

## premise

- **音标**: 'premis
- **释义**: n. 前提, 房屋连地基, 上述各项\nvt. 预先提出, 引出, 作为...的前提\nvi. 作出前提

## preposition

- **音标**: .prepә'ziʃәn
- **释义**: n. 前置词, 介词
- **英文释义**: A word that shows the relationship between a noun or pronoun and other words in a sentence, typically indicating direction, location, time, or method.
- **词根词缀**: pre(前)+pos(放)+ition(名词)→放在前面的词→介词
- **例句**:
- The book is on the table — 'on' is a preposition indicating location.
- She succeeded despite numerous obstacles — 'despite' functions as a preposition introducing a noun phrase.
- **考频**: 1

- **词根词缀**: pre(前)+scrib(写)+e→预先写→规定
- **例句**:

## pretext

- **音标**: 'pri:tekst
- **释义**: n. 借口, 托词\nv. 以...为借口
- **英文释义**: a reason given to justify an action or decision, especially one that is not the real reason
- **词根词缀**: pre(前)+text(编织)→预先编织的→借口
- **例句**:
- He used a scheduling conflict as a pretext to avoid attending the meeting.
- The government imposed new restrictions on civil liberties under the pretext of national security.
- **考频**: 4
