# 分散与转移

> **类型**: 近义

> **学习提示**: 表示使事物散开、转移注意力或驱散的动词，注意 distract 常用于注意力。

**5 词**

---

## disperse

- **音标**: dis'pә:s
- **释义**: vt. 分散, 传播, 散开\nvi. 分散\na. 分散的\n[计] 分散
- **英文释义**: to cause something to break up and move in different directions; to spread over a wide area
- **词根词缀**: dis(分开)+sperse(散开)→散开→分散
- **例句**:
- The police used tear gas to disperse the crowd that had gathered outside the parliament building.
- New technologies have helped disperse knowledge more widely across developing regions.
- **考频**: 9
## dissipate

- **音标**: 'disipeit
- **释义**: vt. 散失, 驱散, 浪费\nvi. 消散, 放荡
- **英文释义**: to disappear or cause something to disappear gradually, especially smoke, fog, energy, or tension
- **词根词缀**: dis(分开)+sip(扔)+ate(动词)→扔开→消散
- **例句**:
- The morning mist began to dissipate as the sun rose.
- Prolonged stress can dissipate mental clarity and impair decision-making ability.
- **考频**: 3
## distract

- **音标**: dis'trækt
- **释义**: vt. 转移, 分心, 使发狂
- **英文释义**: to draw someone's attention away from something important or serious
- **词根词缀**: dis(离开)+tract(拉)→拉开注意力→分散
- **例句**:
- The constant notifications from social media distract students from their exam preparation.
- Her anxiety about the upcoming presentation distracted her so much that she forgot to include key data in the report.
- **考频**: 44
## diffuse

- **音标**: di'fju:z
- **释义**: v. 散播, 传播\na. 散开的, 弥漫的
- **英文释义**: v. to spread out over a wide area; a. not concentrated, widely dispersed
- **词根词缀**: dis(分开)+fus(流)+e→流开→扩散
- **例句**:
- The government launched a campaign to diffuse accurate health information across rural areas.
- Her lecture was criticized for being too diffuse, lacking a clear central argument.
- **考频**: 6
## displace

- **音标**: dis'pleis
- **释义**: vt. 移置, 替换, 转移
- **英文释义**: to remove something or someone from the usual or proper place and replace them with something or someone else
- **词根词缀**: dis(否定)+place(位置)→移开位置→取代
- **例句**:
- The construction of the dam displaced over 10,000 residents from their ancestral villages.
- Automation is increasingly displacing low-skilled workers in manufacturing industries.
- **考频**: 12
