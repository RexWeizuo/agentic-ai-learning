# 暗示/指示

> **类型**: 近义词

> **学习提示**: imply 是动词 (暗示)；implication 是名词 (含义/牵连)；implicit 是形容词 (含蓄的/盲从的)。

**7 词**

---

## hint

- **音标**: hint
- **释义**: n. 暗示, 提示\nv. 暗示, 示意
- **英文释义**: a slight indication or suggestion of something, often indirect or subtle
- **词根词缀**: hint(暗示)→暗示
- **例句**:
- The professor dropped a hint that the final exam would focus on postcolonial theory.
- She hinted at her dissatisfaction with the proposal by remaining silent during the discussion.
- **考频**: 23
## implication

- **音标**: .impli'keiʃәn
- **释义**: n. 牵连, 含义, 暗示\n[法] 推断, 含蓄之意, 暗示
- **英文释义**: something that is suggested or implied without being directly stated; a possible consequence or connection
- **词根词缀**: im(进入)+plic(折叠)+ation(名词)→折叠进去→暗示
- **例句**:
- The implication of his resignation was that the company's financial problems were more serious than admitted.
- She denied any implication that she had acted unethically, insisting her comments were taken out of context.
- **考频**: 72
## implicit

- **音标**: im'plisit
- **释义**: a. 暗示的, 含蓄的, 固有的, 绝对的\n[经] 不讲明的, 含蓄的
- **英文释义**: implied though not directly expressed; inherent or essential as a natural part of something
- **词根词缀**: im(进入)+plic(折叠)+it(形容词)→折叠在内的→含蓄的
- **例句**:
- The contract contained implicit terms that were never written but understood by both parties.
- Her silence was not indifference—it carried an implicit criticism of the proposal.
- **考频**: 14
## imply

- **音标**: im'plai
- **释义**: vt. 暗示, 意味\n[计] 隐含
- **英文释义**: to suggest or indicate something without stating it directly
- **词根词缀**: im(进入)+ply(折叠)→折叠进去→暗示
- **例句**:
- His silence implied disagreement with the proposal.
- The data imply a strong correlation between sleep deprivation and cognitive decline.
- **考频**: 135
## indicate

- **音标**: 'indikeit
- **释义**: vt. 显示, 象征, 指示, 指出\n[医] 指示
- **英文释义**: to show, suggest, or point out something clearly
- **词根词缀**: in(加强)+dic(说)+ate(动词)→说出→指示
- **例句**:
- The data indicate a significant rise in global temperatures over the past decade.
- Her pale complexion and fatigue indicated that she was suffering from anemia.
- **考频**: 209
## indication

- **音标**: .indi'keiʃәn
- **释义**: n. 指示, 象征, 暗示\n[医] 指示, 指征, 适应征
- **英文释义**: a sign or piece of evidence that suggests something is true or exists
- **词根词缀**: in(加强)+dic(说)+ation(名词)→指示
- **例句**:
- The doctor noted several clinical indications of liver dysfunction, including jaundice and elevated enzyme levels.
- There is no clear indication that the policy has improved economic growth, despite initial optimism.
- **考频**: 21
## indicative

- **音标**: in'dikәtiv
- **释义**: a. 指示的, 象征的, 表示...的\n[经] 指标的
- **英文释义**: serving as a sign or indication of something
- **词根词缀**: in(加强)+dic(说)+ative(形容词)→指示的
- **例句**:
- The sharp rise in unemployment is indicative of a weakening economy.
- Her reluctance to answer the question was indicative of guilt.
- **考频**: 10
