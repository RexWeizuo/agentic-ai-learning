## fancy

- **音标**: 'fænsi
- **释义**: n. 想象力, 幻想, 喜好\na. 想象的, 精美的, 新奇的, 奇特的, 高价的, 特级的\nvt. 想象, 设想, 相信, 喜爱\nvi. 想象, 幻想
- **英文释义**: n. imagination or whimsical desire; a liking for something; a. elaborate, ornate, or extravagant; vt. to imagine, believe, or like
- **词根词缀**: fan(出现)+cy(名词)→幻想
- **例句**:
- Her novel is full of fanciful descriptions that blur the line between reality and illusion.
- He didn’t fancy the idea of working overtime on weekends, despite the generous pay.
- **考频**: 37
## fantastic

- **音标**: fæn'tæstik
- **释义**: a. 奇妙的, 稀奇的, 空想的
- **英文释义**: extremely good, impressive, or unusual; also: existing only in imagination or fantasy
- **词根词缀**: fant(幻想)+astic(形容词)→幻想的→极好的
- **例句**:
- The research team achieved a fantastic breakthrough in renewable energy storage.
- Her proposal was imaginative but ultimately fantastic, lacking empirical support.
- **考频**: 16
## fantasy

- **音标**: 'fæntәsi
- **释义**: n. 幻想, 想象的产物\n[医] 幻想
- **英文释义**: A mental image or series of thoughts about something that is not real or has not happened, often involving imagination or desire.
- **词根词缀**: fant(幻想)+asy(名词)→幻想
- **例句**:
- Her novel blends historical fact with fantasy to create a compelling alternate world.
- The patient's persistent belief in being monitored by aliens was diagnosed as a pathological fantasy.
- **考频**: 17
## fiction

- **音标**: 'fikʃәn
- **释义**: n. 小说, 虚构故事\n[法] 虚构的事实, 捏造, 拟制
- **英文释义**: literary works, especially novels and short stories, that describe imaginary people and events
- **词根词缀**: fict(虚构)+ion(名词)→虚构的事物→小说
- **例句**:
- Much of what was once considered science fiction is now becoming scientific reality.
- The distinction between historical fact and fictional narrative is often blurred in postmodern literature.
- **考频**: 103
