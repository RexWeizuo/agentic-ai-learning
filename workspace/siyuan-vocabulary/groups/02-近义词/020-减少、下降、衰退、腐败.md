# 减少、下降、衰退、腐败

> **类型**: 近义

> **学习提示**: 表示数量减少、质量下降或道德/物质的腐坏。

**6 词**

---

## decrease

- **音标**: 'di:kri:s
- **释义**: n. 减少, 减少量\nv. 减少
- **英文释义**: to become or make something smaller in size, amount, or degree
- **词根词缀**: de(向下)+creas(生长)+e→向下生长→减少
- **例句**:
- The government implemented new policies to decrease carbon emissions.
- A significant decrease in consumer spending has raised concerns about an impending recession.
- **考频**: 118
## decline

- **音标**: di'klain
- **释义**: n. 衰退, 跌落, 下降\nvt. 使降低, 婉谢\nvi. 下降, 衰落, 偏斜
- **英文释义**: a gradual decrease in strength, quality, or number; to refuse politely or to become weaker or less successful
- **词根词缀**: de(向下)+clin(倾斜)+e→向下倾斜→下降
- **例句**:
- The company's profits have been in steady decline for three consecutive quarters.
- She declined the invitation to speak at the conference, citing a prior commitment.
- **考频**: 305
## decay

- **音标**: di'kei
- **释义**: n. 衰退, 腐败\nv. (使)衰退, (使)腐败
- **英文释义**: the process of becoming gradually weaker, worse, or less effective; or to rot or decompose
- **词根词缀**: de(向下)+cay(落下)→衰落
- **例句**:
- The ancient temple fell into decay after centuries of neglect.
- Economic instability accelerated the decay of the country's infrastructure.
- **考频**: 20
## degenerate

- **音标**: di'dʒenәreit
- **释义**: a. 堕落的\nvi. 退化, 堕落\nvt. 使变质, 使退化
- **英文释义**: to decline or deteriorate in quality, function, or morality; to become worse over time
- **词根词缀**: de(向下)+gener(种族)+ate(动词)→种族下降→退化
- **例句**:
- The political debate quickly degenerated into personal insults.
- Chronic inflammation can cause healthy tissue to degenerate into fibrous scar tissue.
- **考频**: 1
## corrode

- **音标**: kә'rәud
- **释义**: vt. 使腐蚀, 侵蚀, 破坏\nvi. 被侵蚀, 起腐蚀作用
- **英文释义**: to gradually destroy or damage a material, especially metal, by a chemical reaction (e.g., with oxygen or acid); to erode or deteriorate over time
- **词根词缀**: co(共同)+rrode
- **例句**:
- Acid rain can corrode ancient stone monuments, causing irreversible damage.
- Prolonged exposure to saltwater will corrode the ship's hull if protective coatings are not regularly maintained.
## corrupt

- **音标**: kә'rʌpt
- **释义**: a. 腐败的, 贪污的, 讹误充斥的\nvt. 使腐烂, 腐蚀, 使恶化\nvi. 腐烂, 堕落
- **英文释义**: morally degenerate or dishonest, especially due to bribery or abuse of power; to cause something to become damaged, inaccurate, or morally unsound
- **词根词缀**: cor(加强)+rupt(断裂)→完全断裂→腐败
- **例句**:
- The investigation revealed that several high-ranking officials were corrupt and had accepted bribes.
- Digital files can become corrupt if the storage device fails during a write operation.
- **考频**: 9
