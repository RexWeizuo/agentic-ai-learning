# 下降与减少

> **类型**: 近义

> **学习提示**: 表示数量减少、位置下降或质量恶化的词汇，dilute 特指浓度降低。

**7 词**

---

## descend

- **音标**: di'send
- **释义**: vi. 下降, 世代相传, 屈尊, 袭击\nvt. 下降
- **英文释义**: to move or fall downward; to pass from a higher to a lower level, rank, or condition
- **词根词缀**: de(向下)+scend(爬)→向下爬→下降
- **例句**:
- The plane began to descend rapidly due to sudden turbulence.
- Hereditary diseases can descend from one generation to the next.
## descent

- **音标**: di'sent
- **释义**: n. 降落, 家系, 侵袭, 血统\n[医] 下降, 世代, 血统
- **英文释义**: the act of moving downward; a person's ancestry or lineage; a sudden attack
- **词根词缀**: de(向下)+scent(爬)→下降
- **例句**:
- The aircraft began its final descent into Heathrow Airport.
- Her political views are deeply rooted in her intellectual descent from Enlightenment thinkers.
- **考频**: 10
## diminish

- **音标**: di'miniʃ
- **释义**: v. (使)减少, (使)变小
- **英文释义**: to make or become smaller, fewer, or less in size, importance, or intensity
- **词根词缀**: de(向下)+min(小)+ish(动词)→向下变小→减少
- **例句**:
- The government's austerity measures are expected to diminish public spending on healthcare.
- As the evidence mounted, the credibility of his argument began to diminish.
- **考频**: 46
## depress

- **音标**: di'pres
- **释义**: vt. 使沮丧, 压低, 降低, 使萧条\n[化] 推下
- **英文释义**: to make someone feel sad and without hope; to reduce in amount, level, or value
- **词根词缀**: de(向下)+press(压)→向下压→压抑
- **例句**:
- The prolonged economic downturn continued to depress consumer confidence.
- Her failure to secure the scholarship deeply depressed her, affecting both her motivation and academic performance.
- **考频**: 2
## drop

- **音标**: drɒp
- **释义**: n. 滴, 微量, 落下, 空投\nvi. 放下, 掉下, 下降\nvt. 使滴下, 放下, 丢失, 遗漏\n[计] 投入, 投入点, 接入点, 分接点
- **英文释义**: A small quantity of liquid that falls or is released in a spherical form; to fall or decrease suddenly; to let something fall or cease involvement.
- **词根词缀**: drop→掉落
- **例句**:
- The doctor instructed the patient to apply one drop of the eye solution twice daily.
- Stock prices began to drop sharply after the unexpected announcement, prompting investors to reevaluate their portfolios.
- **考频**: 211
## dilute

- **音标**: dai'lju:t
- **释义**: vt. 冲淡, 稀释\na. 淡的, 稀释的
- **英文释义**: to make a liquid weaker or less concentrated by adding water or another solvent
- **词根词缀**: di(分开)+lut(洗)+e→洗开→稀释
- **例句**:
- The scientist diluted the acid with distilled water before conducting the experiment.
- Excessive media coverage can dilute the impact of the original message in policy debates.
- **考频**: 3
## deteriorate

- **音标**: di'tiәriәreit
- **释义**: v. (使)恶化
- **英文释义**: to become progressively worse in quality, condition, or value
- **词根词缀**: de(向下)+ter(土地)+ior(比较级)+ate(动词)→土地变得更差→恶化
- **例句**:
- The patient's health began to deteriorate rapidly after the surgery.
- Economic conditions in the region have continued to deteriorate due to political instability.
- **考频**: 19
