# 限制、抑制与压抑

> **类型**: 近义

> **学习提示**: restrain/restraint 侧重自我克制或物理束缚，restrict 侧重规则限制，repression 侧重心理或政治压抑。

**4 词**

---

## repression

- **音标**: ri'preʃәn
- **释义**: n. 抑制, 压抑, 制止\n[医] 压抑, 抑制
- **英文释义**: The act of holding back or preventing something, especially an emotion, impulse, or memory, often unconsciously; in psychology, the exclusion of distressing thoughts from conscious awareness.
- **词根词缀**: re(回)+press(压)+ion(名词)→压回→镇压
- **例句**:
- Freud argued that repression of traumatic childhood memories can lead to psychological disorders later in life.
- The government's repression of dissenting voices undermined its legitimacy and fueled public unrest.
- **考频**: 6
## restrain

- **音标**: ri'strein
- **释义**: vt. 抑制, 阻止, 束缚\n[法] 抑制, 遏制, 制止
- **英文释义**: to hold back, control, or prevent someone or something from acting or happening
- **词根词缀**: re(回)+strain(拉紧)→拉回→抑制
- **例句**:
- The government imposed new regulations to restrain the rapid growth of speculative investment.
- She struggled to restrain her anger during the heated debate.
- **考频**: 18
## restraint

- **音标**: ri'streint
- **释义**: n. 抑制, 克制, 束缚\n[医] 约束, 拘束
- **英文释义**: The act of controlling one's emotions, behavior, or impulses; a limitation or restriction imposed on something.
- **词根词缀**: re(回)+strain(拉紧)+t(名词)→抑制
- **例句**:
- Her remarkable restraint in the face of provocation earned her colleagues' respect.
- The court imposed legal restraints on the company's ability to acquire competitors.
- **考频**: 16
## restrict

- **音标**: ri'strikt
- **释义**: vt. 限制, 限定, 约束\n[医] 限制
- **英文释义**: to limit something by rules, laws, or conditions
- **词根词缀**: re(回)+strict(拉紧)→限制
- **例句**:
- The government has imposed new regulations to restrict the use of single-use plastics.
- Medical guidelines often restrict the prescription of certain antibiotics to prevent drug resistance.
- **考频**: 52
