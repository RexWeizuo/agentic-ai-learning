# 模仿/虚构/假象

> **类型**: 近义词

> **学习提示**: illusion 指错觉或幻想；hypocrisy 指伪善、虚伪。

**4 词**

---

## imitate

- **音标**: 'imiteit
- **释义**: vt. 模仿, 效法, 冒充, 仿造
- **英文释义**: to copy the behavior, appearance, or sound of someone or something
- **词根词缀**: imit(模仿)+ate→模仿
- **例句**:
- Children often imitate the speech and gestures of adults.
- The new policy imitates successful models from Scandinavian countries.
- **考频**: 46
## imitation

- **音标**: .imi'teiʃәn
- **释义**: n. 模仿, 效法, 冒充, 赝品\n[医] 模仿, 仿制, 仿制品
- **英文释义**: the act of copying someone or something, often in behavior, appearance, or style; a copy that is not genuine
- **词根词缀**: imit(模仿)+ation(名词)→模仿
- **例句**:
- Her flawless imitation of the CEO's speech impressed the entire board.
- The museum refused to display the painting after experts confirmed it was an imitation rather than an original Rembrandt.
- **考频**: 22
## illusion

- **音标**: i'lju:ʒәn
- **释义**: n. 幻影, 错觉, 幻想\n[医] 错觉
- **英文释义**: A false idea or belief, especially one based on a misinterpretation of sensory perception.
- **词根词缀**: il(进入)+lus(玩)+ion→进入玩耍→幻觉
- **例句**:
- The mirage in the desert was a powerful optical illusion that made travelers believe there was water ahead.
- Her confidence in the company's long-term stability turned out to be an illusion after the sudden bankruptcy.
- **考频**: 21
## hypocrisy

- **音标**: hi'pɒkrәsi
- **释义**: n. 伪善\n[法] 伪善, 虚伪
- **英文释义**: the practice of claiming to have moral standards or beliefs to which one's own behavior does not conform
- **词根词缀**: hypo(在...下)+cris(判断)+y→在表面下判断→虚伪
- **例句**:
- His criticism of corporate greed rang hollow, revealing a deep hypocrisy given his own offshore tax shelters.
- The politician's sudden advocacy for fiscal responsibility was widely seen as hypocrisy after years of supporting wasteful spending.
- **考频**: 2
