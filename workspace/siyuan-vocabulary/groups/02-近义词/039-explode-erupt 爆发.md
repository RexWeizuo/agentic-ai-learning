# explode/erupt 爆发

> **类型**: 近义

> **学习提示**: 爆发、爆炸相关词汇

**4 词**

---

## erupt

- **音标**: i'rʌpt
- **释义**: vi. 爆发\nvt. 喷出
- **英文释义**: to break out suddenly and violently, especially with force or intensity (intransitive); to expel or discharge something abruptly (transitive)
- **词根词缀**: e(出)+rupt(破)→破裂而出→爆发
- **例句**:
- Tensions between the two countries erupted into open conflict after the border incident.
- The volcano erupted, spewing ash and lava over the surrounding region.
- **考频**: 12
## explode

- **音标**: ik'splәud
- **释义**: vi. 爆炸, 爆发, 激增\nvt. 使爆炸
- **英文释义**: to burst violently with a loud noise; to increase or develop very rapidly; to cause something to explode
- **词根词缀**: ex(出)+plod(鼓掌)+e→鼓掌而出→爆炸
- **例句**:
- The political crisis exploded into nationwide protests within days.
- Inflation exploded after the central bank abandoned its monetary controls.
- **考频**: 10
## explosion

- **音标**: ik'splәuʒәn
- **释义**: n. 爆发, 激增, 爆炸(声)\n[化] 爆炸
- **英文释义**: a sudden, violent release of energy, often with heat, light, and loud noise; or a rapid, dramatic increase in number, size, or intensity
- **词根词缀**: ex(向外)+plos(爆炸)+ion(名词)→向外爆炸→爆炸
- **例句**:
- The explosion at the chemical plant caused widespread damage and forced the evacuation of nearby residents.
- There has been an explosion in demand for renewable energy technologies over the past decade.
- **考频**: 40
## explosive

- **音标**: ik'splәusiv
- **释义**: n. 炸药, 爆破音\na. 易爆发的, 爆炸的, 暴躁的
- **英文释义**: A substance that explodes violently; or something characterized by sudden, intense, or uncontrolled energy, emotion, or growth.
- **词根词缀**: ex(向外)+plos(爆炸)+ive(形容词)→能向外爆炸的→爆炸性的
- **例句**:
- The engineers used a controlled explosive to demolish the old bridge safely.
- Her explosive temper made her colleagues hesitant to raise controversial issues in meetings.
- **考频**: 12
