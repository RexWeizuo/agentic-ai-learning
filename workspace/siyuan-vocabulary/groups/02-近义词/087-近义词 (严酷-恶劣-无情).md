# 近义词 (严酷/恶劣/无情)

> **类型**: 近义

> **学习提示**: 形容环境、态度或物品的恶劣。savage(野蛮的), ruthless(无情的), shabby(破旧的/低劣的), sharp(尖锐的/剧烈的)。

**4 词**

---

## savage

- **音标**: 'sævidʒ
- **释义**: a. 未开化的, 野蛮的, 无礼的, 残酷的, 原始的, 粗鲁的, 未开发的\nvt. 用暴力对付, 凶猛攻击\nn. 原始人, 粗鲁人
- **英文释义**: adj. uncivilized, violent, cruel, or extremely rude; n. a primitive or brutally uncultured person; vt. to attack fiercely
- **词根词缀**: sav(森林)+age(名词后缀)→野蛮的
- **例句**:
- The documentary exposed the savage exploitation of natural resources in war-torn regions.
- Her critique was so savage that several reviewers accused her of lacking academic objectivity.
- **考频**: 8
## ruthless

- **音标**: 'ru:θlis
- **释义**: a. 无情的, 残忍的, 毫不留情的, 彻底的\n[法] 无情的, 残忍的
- **英文释义**: having no pity or compassion; cruel and determined to achieve one's goals at any cost
- **词根词缀**: ruth(怜悯)+less(无)→无情的
- **例句**:
- The company launched a ruthless cost-cutting campaign, laying off hundreds of employees without warning.
- Her ruthless ambition drove her to undermine colleagues and seize every opportunity for promotion.
- **考频**: 1
## shabby

- **音标**: 'ʃæbi
- **释义**: a. 衣衫褴褛的, 低劣的, 破旧的, 吝啬的, 卑鄙的
- **英文释义**: worn-out, shoddy, or unfairly treated; lacking in quality, decency, or generosity
- **词根词缀**: shab(破旧)+by(形容词)→破旧的
- **例句**:
- The candidate's shabby treatment of staff during the campaign damaged his credibility.
- Her shabby coat and scuffed shoes contrasted sharply with the elegant surroundings of the conference hall.
- **考频**: 5
## sharp

- **音标**: ʃɑ:p
- **释义**: n. 半升音调, 利刃, 骗子\na. 锋利的, 明显的, 敏锐的, 急剧的, 尖刻的, 严厉的, 刺耳的, 精明的\nadv. 锐利地, 急速地
- **英文释义**: having a thin edge or point that can cut or pierce; also used to describe something sudden, clear, intense, or intellectually acute
- **词根词缀**: sharp(锋利)→锋利的
- **例句**:
- The surgeon made a sharp incision with the scalpel.
- There was a sharp decline in exports following the policy change.
- **考频**: 61
