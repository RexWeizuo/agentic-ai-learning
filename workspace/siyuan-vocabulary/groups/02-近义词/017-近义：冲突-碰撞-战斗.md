# 近义：冲突/碰撞/战斗

> **类型**: 近义

> **学习提示**: clash(冲突/撞击声), collide/collision(物理碰撞或利益抵触), combat(战斗/格斗)。

**4 词**

---

## clash

- **音标**: klæʃ
- **释义**: n. 冲突, 撞击声, 抵触\nvi. 冲突, 抵触\nvt. 使发出撞击声\n[计] 对撞
- **英文释义**: a sudden, loud noise from objects hitting each other; a sharp disagreement or conflict between people, ideas, or interests
- **词根词缀**: clash(碰撞声)→冲突
- **例句**:
- The clash between traditional values and modern technology is increasingly evident in policy debates.
- When the two factions met, their ideological differences led to an immediate and heated clash.
- **考频**: 10
## collide

- **音标**: kә'laid
- **释义**: vi. 碰撞, 互撞, 抵触
- **英文释义**: to crash into something or someone, especially violently; to come into conflict or opposition
- **词根词缀**: col(共同)+lid(碰撞)+e→碰撞→冲突
- **例句**:
- The two cars collided at the intersection due to heavy fog.
- Her pragmatic approach to policy often collides with his idealistic principles.
- **考频**: 12
## collision

- **音标**: kә'liʒәn
- **释义**: n. 碰撞, 冲突, 抵触\n[计] 冲突
- **英文释义**: A violent encounter between two or more moving objects, or a clash of ideas, interests, or systems.
- **词根词缀**: col(共同)+lis(碰撞)+ion(名词)→碰撞→冲突
- **例句**:
- The collision between the two cars resulted in serious injuries and a major traffic jam.
- There is an inevitable collision between economic growth and environmental sustainability in many developing countries.
- **考频**: 17
## combat

- **音标**: 'kɒmbæt
- **释义**: n. 争斗, 战斗\nvi. 战斗, 争斗\nvt. 与...战斗, 与...斗争
- **英文释义**: to fight against something harmful or undesirable; a physical struggle or armed conflict
- **词根词缀**: com(共同)+bat(打)→共同打→战斗
- **例句**:
- The government has launched a nationwide campaign to combat climate change.
- Soldiers were trained to combat enemy forces in urban environments.
- **考频**: 36
