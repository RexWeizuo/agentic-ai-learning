# 揭示与探测

> **类型**: 近义

> **学习提示**: 表示发现隐藏事物、揭露真相或通过手段引出信息的动词及派生词。

**6 词**

---

## detect

- **音标**: di'tekt
- **释义**: vt. 发现, 察觉, 探测\n[法] 发现, 查明, 探测
- **英文释义**: to discover or notice something, especially something that is not easy to see, hear, or understand
- **词根词缀**: de(去除)+tect(掩盖)→去除掩盖→发现
- **例句**:
- The new sensor can detect even minute traces of toxic gas.
- Experienced auditors are trained to detect inconsistencies in financial statements.
- **考频**: 66
## detective

- **音标**: di'tektiv
- **释义**: n. 侦探\na. 侦探的
- **英文释义**: a person, especially a police officer, whose job is to investigate crimes and gather evidence
- **词根词缀**: de(去除)+tect(掩盖)+ive(形容词)→去除掩盖的→侦探的
- **例句**:
- The detective meticulously reconstructed the timeline of the crime using surveillance footage and witness statements.
- As a seasoned detective, she had an uncanny ability to detect inconsistencies in suspects' alibis.
- **考频**: 15
## detector

- **音标**: di'tektә
- **释义**: n. 发现者, 检验器, 检波器\n[计] 检波器; 检测器
- **英文释义**: A device or instrument that detects, identifies, or measures the presence of something (e.g., radiation, smoke, signals, or substances).
- **词根词缀**: de(去除)+tect(掩盖)+or(名词)→去除掩盖的东西→探测器
- **例句**:
- The airport installed advanced metal detectors to enhance passenger security.
- In digital communication systems, a detector is essential for extracting the original signal from a modulated carrier wave.
- **考频**: 8
## disclose

- **音标**: dis'klәuz
- **释义**: vt. 揭露, 透露\n[法] 揭发, 揭露, 公开
- **英文释义**: to make something known or reveal information that was previously secret or unknown
- **词根词缀**: dis(否定)+close(关闭)→不关闭→揭露
- **例句**:
- The company refused to disclose the financial details to the public.
- Under cross-examination, the witness was compelled to disclose confidential communications with her client.
- **考频**: 15
## discern

- **音标**: di'zә:n
- **释义**: v. 辨别, 看清楚, 了解
- **英文释义**: to perceive or recognize something clearly, often with difficulty or after careful attention
- **词根词缀**: dis(分开)+cern(区分)→区分开→辨别
- **例句**:
- It is difficult to discern any meaningful pattern in the data without further analysis.
- She could barely discern his figure in the fog, but she knew it was him by his posture.
- **考频**: 6
## elicit

- **音标**: i'lisit
- **释义**: vt. 引出, 推导出, 引起
- **英文释义**: to draw out or bring forth (a response, reaction, or information) from someone, often with effort or skill
- **词根词缀**: e(出)+lic(引诱)+it→引出→诱出
- **例句**:
- The teacher's open-ended question was designed to elicit thoughtful responses from students.
- Researchers used carefully worded interviews to elicit honest feedback about the policy's real-world impact.
- **考频**: 9
