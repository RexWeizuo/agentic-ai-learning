# 坚固与稳定

> **类型**: 近义

> **学习提示**: 表示稳固、不易动摇的形容词。sturdy (强健的/结实的), stiff (坚硬的/生硬的)。

**5 词**

---

## stability

- **音标**: stә'biliti
- **释义**: n. 安定, 稳定性, 坚实, 巩固\n[化] 稳定性
- **英文释义**: the state of being stable, especially resistance to change or disturbance
- **词根词缀**: st(站立)+ability(能力)→稳定(性)
- **例句**:
- The government's priority is to restore economic stability after the financial crisis.
- Chemical stability is crucial for the safe storage and transport of reactive substances.
- **考频**: 48
## stable

- **音标**: 'steibl
- **释义**: n. 马房, 牛棚\na. 稳定的, 安定的, 坚固的, 坚定的\nvt. 赶入马房\nvi. 被关在马厩
- **英文释义**: n. a building for housing horses; a. firmly established, not likely to change or deteriorate; vt./vi. to put or be put into a stable
- **词根词缀**: st(站立)+able(能)→稳定的
- **例句**:
- The government has taken measures to maintain a stable economy amid global uncertainty.
- After the race, the jockey led the exhausted horse back to the stable.
- **考频**: 61
## steady

- **音标**: 'stedi
- **释义**: a. 稳定的, 不动摇的, 沉着的, 稳固的, 坚定的, 经常的\nvt. 使稳定, 使坚定\nvi. 变为沉着, 稳固
- **英文释义**: firm, unwavering, or constant in position, condition, or purpose; not easily disturbed or changed
- **词根词缀**: stead(代替)+y(形容词)→稳定的
- **例句**:
- The economy showed steady growth despite global uncertainties.
- She took a deep breath to steady her nerves before the presentation.
- **考频**: 40
## sturdy

- **音标**: 'stә:di
- **释义**: a. 强健的, 健全的\n[医] 羊晕倒病, 羊蹒跚病
- **英文释义**: strongly built and healthy; robust
- **词根词缀**: stur(坚固)+dy(形容词)→坚固的
- **例句**:
- The old oak table is remarkably sturdy and has survived decades of heavy use.
- She maintained a sturdy constitution despite years of demanding clinical work.
- **考频**: 5
## stiff

- **音标**: stif
- **释义**: a. 坚硬的, 严厉的, 呆板的, 生硬的, 刚强的, 强烈的, 粘的, 稠的, 艰难的\nn. 死尸, 醉鬼, 钞票\nvt. 不肯付...小费
- **英文释义**: firm and difficult to bend or move; rigid in manner or attitude; intense or severe; (slang) a dead body, a drunk person, or money; (vt, informal) to refuse to tip
- **词根词缀**: stiff→僵硬的
- **例句**:
- The stiff collar made it uncomfortable for him to turn his head during the long lecture.
- She gave a stiff, formal reply to the interviewer’s question, revealing little enthusiasm.
- **考频**: 5
