## evolution

- **音标**: .i:vә'lu:ʃәn
- **释义**: n. 进化, 发展, 进展, (气体)放出, 开方\n[医] 进化, 演化, 旋出
- **英文释义**: the gradual development and change of something over time, especially the process by which different kinds of living organisms have developed from earlier forms
- **词根词缀**: e(出)+volut(转)+ion(名词)→转出来→进化
- **例句**:
- The theory of evolution by natural selection was first proposed by Charles Darwin.
- Technological evolution has dramatically transformed the way we communicate and work.
- **考频**: 95
## evolve

- **音标**: i'vɒlv
- **释义**: vi. 进展, 进化, 展开\nvt. 使发展, 使推断出, 使进化
- **英文释义**: to develop gradually, especially over a long period of time, into a more complex or advanced state
- **词根词缀**: e(出)+volv(转)+e→转出来→进化
- **例句**:
- The human brain has evolved to process language more efficiently than that of other primates.
- As the project progressed, its goals continued to evolve in response to new data and stakeholder feedback.
- **考频**: 65
## evoke

- **音标**: i'vәuk
- **释义**: vt. 唤起, 引起, 召(魂)\n[法] 提审, 移送
- **英文释义**: to bring a feeling, memory, or image into the mind
- **词根词缀**: e(出)+vok(叫)+e→叫出来→唤起
- **例句**:
- The old photograph evoked vivid memories of her childhood.
- His speech evoked a strong sense of national pride among the audience.
- **考频**: 12
## evil

- **音标**: 'i:vl
- **释义**: n. 邪恶, 不幸, 罪恶\na. 邪恶的, 不幸的, 有害的, 讨厌的
- **英文释义**: profoundly immoral and wicked; causing harm or suffering
- **词根词缀**: e(出)+vil(邪恶)→邪恶
- **例句**:
- The novel explores the eternal struggle between good and evil.
- Policymakers must address the root causes of social evil, such as poverty and inequality.
- **考频**: 63
