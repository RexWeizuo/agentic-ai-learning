# es- 本质/建立

## essay

- **音标**: 'esei. e'sei
- **释义**: n. 随笔, 短文, 评论, 企图\nvt. 试图
- **英文释义**: A short piece of writing on a particular subject, often expressing the author's personal viewpoint; or to make an attempt, especially in a formal or tentative way.
- **词根词缀**: es(出)+say(说)→说出来→散文
- **例句**:
- Her essay on climate policy was praised for its clarity and original argument.
- The candidate essayed a new approach to urban planning, though it remained untested.
- **考频**: 347
## essence

- **音标**: 'esns
- **释义**: n. 实质, 本质, 香精\n[化] 香精
- **英文释义**: the most important or fundamental nature or quality of something
- **词根词缀**: ess(存在)+ence(名词)→存在的东西→本质
- **例句**:
- The essence of democracy is the participation of citizens in decision-making.
- Her latest novel captures the essence of urban alienation in modern society.
- **考频**: 28
## essential

- **音标**: i'senʃәl
- **释义**: n. 要素, 要点, 本质\na. 必要的, 重要的, 本质的\n[计] 本质冒险
- **英文释义**: absolutely necessary; extremely important; fundamental to the nature of something
- **词根词缀**: ess(存在)+ential(形容词)→本质的
- **例句**:
- Water is an essential nutrient for human survival.
- A strong foundation in grammar is essential for mastering academic writing.
- **考频**: 209
## establish

- **音标**: i'stæbliʃ
- **释义**: vt. 建立, 确立, 制定\nvi. 移植生长
- **英文释义**: to set up or create something that is intended to last, such as an organization, system, or belief
- **词根词缀**: e(加强)+stabl(稳定)+ish(动词)→使稳定→建立
- **例句**:
- The government has established a new regulatory framework to oversee artificial intelligence development.
- It took several years for the species to establish itself in the new ecosystem after being introduced.
- **考频**: 153
## estate

- **音标**: i'steit
- **释义**: n. 不动产, (人生的)阶段, 阶层, 财产\n[经] 房地产, 遗产, 财产
- **英文释义**: 1. A large piece of land with buildings, often including a mansion; 2. A person's total property and assets, especially after death; 3. A social class or rank in a society.
- **词根词缀**: e(出)+stat(站)+e→站出来的东西→财产
- **例句**:
- The government imposed new taxes on high-value real estate to curb speculation.
- Under the terms of the will, she inherited the entire estate, including both property and financial assets.
- **考频**: 66
## esteem

- **音标**: i'sti:m
- **释义**: n. 尊敬, 尊重\nvt. 尊敬, 尊重, 认为
- **英文释义**: respect and admiration for someone or something
- **词根词缀**: e(出)+steem(价值)→给出价值→尊重
- **例句**:
- Her colleagues hold her in high esteem for her integrity and expertise.
- The judge is widely esteemed for his impartiality and profound legal knowledge.
- **考频**: 32
