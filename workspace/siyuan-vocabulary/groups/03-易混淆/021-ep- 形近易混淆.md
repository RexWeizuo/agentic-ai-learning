# ep- 形近易混淆

> **类型**: 易混淆

> **学习提示**: ep- 开头，拼写相近但意思完全不同

## epic

- **音标**: 'epik
- **释义**: n. 史诗, 叙事诗\na. 史诗的, 叙事诗的
- **英文释义**: A long narrative poem recounting heroic deeds; also used adjectivally to describe something grand, heroic, or monumental.
- **词根词缀**: epi(在...之上)+ic(形容词)→在史诗之上的→史诗的
- **例句**:
- Homer's 'Iliad' is one of the greatest epics in Western literature.
- The team's comeback victory after trailing by 30 points was an epic display of resilience and determination.
- **考频**: 5
## epidemic

- **音标**: .epi'demik
- **释义**: n. 传染病, 流行病\na. 流行的, 传染性的
- **英文释义**: a widespread occurrence of an infectious disease in a community or region at a particular time
- **词根词缀**: epi(在...之间)+dem(人民)+ic(形容词)→在人民之间的→流行的
- **例句**:
- The WHO declared the outbreak a global epidemic after cases surged across five continents.
- Obesity has reached epidemic proportions in many developed countries, prompting urgent public health interventions.
- **考频**: 41
## episode

- **音标**: 'episәud
- **释义**: n. 插曲, 插话, 有趣的事件, 一段情节\n[医] 插话, 插曲, 发作
- **英文释义**: a single incident or event, especially one that is part of a series or has a distinct beginning and end
- **词根词缀**: epi(在...之间)+sode(进入)→进入之间→插曲
- **例句**:
- The latest episode of the political scandal has reignited public debate.
- Her depression worsened during each manic episode, requiring hospitalization.
- **考频**: 21
## epoch

- **音标**: 'i:pɒk
- **释义**: n. 新纪元, 时代, 时期
- **英文释义**: a distinct period of time in history or a person's life, marked by notable events or developments
- **词根词缀**: epi(在...之上)+och(持有)→持有时间→时代
- **例句**:
- The invention of the internet marked a new epoch in global communication.
- The Renaissance was an epoch of extraordinary artistic and intellectual flourishing in Europe.
- **考频**: 4
