# 易混淆：discreet与dessert系列

> **类型**: 易混淆

> **学习提示**: 拼写相近但词义截然不同。discreet(谨慎的) vs discrete(离散的)；dessert(甜点) vs deserve(应得)。

**5 词**

---

## discreet

- **音标**: dis'kri:t
- **释义**: a. 小心的, 慎重的
- **英文释义**: careful and prudent, especially to avoid embarrassment or attract attention
- **词根词缀**: dis(分开)+creet(分辨)→能分辨的→谨慎的
- **例句**:
- She was discreet about her personal life, never discussing it in the office.
- The diplomat made a discreet inquiry into the matter, avoiding any public statement.
- **考频**: 3
## discrete

- **音标**: dis'kri:t
- **释义**: a. 不连续的, 离散的\n[计] 离散的
- **英文释义**: separate and distinct; not continuous
- **词根词缀**: dis(分开)+crete(生长)→分开生长→离散的
- **例句**:
- The data was organized into discrete categories for statistical analysis.
- In digital signal processing, information is represented using discrete values rather than a continuous range.
- **考频**: 1
## discretion

- **音标**: dis'kreʃәn
- **释义**: n. 慎重, 辨别力, 考虑, 处理权\n[法] 有决定权的
- **英文释义**: The ability to make responsible and careful decisions, especially in sensitive situations
- **词根词缀**: dis(分开)+cret(分辨)+ion(名词)→分辨的能力→谨慎
- **例句**:
- Judges have wide discretion in sentencing offenders.
- The manager exercised discretion in approving the expense report, considering the employee's exceptional circumstances.
- **考频**: 4
## dessert

- **音标**: di'zә:t
- **释义**: n. 餐后甜点
- **英文释义**: A sweet course typically served at the end of a meal.
- **词根词缀**: des(去掉)+sert(服务)→最后一道菜→甜点
- **例句**:
- She declined the main course to save room for dessert.
- The restaurant's signature dessert—a deconstructed lemon tart—received widespread critical acclaim.
- **考频**: 10
## deserve

- **音标**: di'zә:v
- **释义**: vt. 该得到, 值得\nvi. 应得报答
- **英文释义**: to be worthy of something, especially a reward, punishment, or recognition
- **词根词缀**: de(加强)+serve(服务)→应得服务→值得
- **例句**:
- Her dedication to the project deserves full recognition.
- He has worked tirelessly for years and truly deserves this promotion.
- **考频**: 80
