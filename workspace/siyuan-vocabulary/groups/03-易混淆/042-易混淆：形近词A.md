# 易混淆：形近词A

> **类型**: 易混淆

> **学习提示**: peel(剥皮)/peep(偷看)/peer(同龄人)；pant(喘息)/pants(裤子)；paste(浆糊)/pasture(牧场)；plain(平原)/plaintiff(原告)。

**10 词**

## peel

- **音标**: pi:l
- **释义**: n. 皮, 外壳\nvt. 削...皮, 剥去...的壳\nvi. 蜕皮, 剥落, 脱皮, 脱去衣服
- **英文释义**: n. the outer layer or skin of a fruit, vegetable, or other object; v. to remove the outer layer or skin from something
- **词根词缀**: peel(词根：剥皮)→剥皮
- **例句**:
- She carefully peeled the orange, avoiding as much pith as possible.
- The paint on the old wall began to peel after years of exposure to moisture.
- **考频**: 2
## peep

- **音标**: pi:p
- **释义**: n. 一瞥, 偷看, 不完全的景象, 初现, 吱吱声, 嘀咕抱怨声, 汽车喇叭声\nvi. 窥视, 出现, 吱吱叫\nvt. 使隐约显现
- **英文释义**: to look quickly or secretly, often through a small opening; also refers to a faint or initial appearance, or a short high-pitched sound
- **词根词缀**: peep
- **例句**:
- She couldn't resist a quick peep at her birthday present before the party.
- As dawn broke, the first peep of sunlight filtered through the clouds.
## peer

- **音标**: piә
- **释义**: n. 同等的人, 匹敌, 贵族\nvi. 凝视, 窥视, 费力地看, 隐现\nvt. 与...同等, 封为贵族
- **英文释义**: n. a person of the same age, status, or ability; a British nobleman; vi. to look closely or with difficulty, often in dim light; vt. to treat or rank as equal
- **词根词缀**: peer(词根：同等)→同等的人
- **例句**:
- She refused to accept advice from her peers, believing she could make better decisions independently.
- The old castle tower peered above the mist, its silhouette barely visible in the dawn light.
- **考频**: 168
## pant

- **音标**: pænt
- **释义**: n. 喘息, 悸动\nvi. 喘息, 渴望\nvt. 气喘吁吁地说
- **英文释义**: to breathe rapidly and with effort, often due to physical exertion or strong emotion; also used to express intense desire
- **词根词缀**: pant(词根：喘气)→喘气
- **例句**:
- After sprinting up the six flights of stairs, she collapsed on the landing, panting heavily.
- He was panting for recognition, his ambition driving him to work relentlessly through the night.
- **考频**: 14
## pants

- **音标**: pænts
- **释义**: n. 裤子, 长裤, 短衬裤, 女式运动短裤
- **英文释义**: a garment covering the body from the waist to the ankles or knees, typically divided into separate parts for each leg
- **词根词缀**: pan(全/泛)+ts
- **例句**:
- She wore tailored black pants to the job interview.
- The athlete changed into moisture-wicking pants before the track meet.
## paste

- **音标**: peist
- **释义**: n. 面团, 面食, 浆糊, 糊状物, 粘贴, 用拳重击\nvt. 用浆糊粘, 张贴, 狠狠地打\n[计] 粘贴
- **英文释义**: A soft, moist mixture or substance; to attach something using adhesive, especially in computing to insert copied data.
- **词根词缀**: paste(词根：糊)→糊状物
- **例句**:
- She used flour and water to make a simple paste for the paper-mâché project.
- After copying the text, press Ctrl+V to paste it into the document.
- **考频**: 3
## pasture

- **音标**: 'pæstʃә
- **释义**: n. 牧场, 草地, 牧草\nvt. 放牧\nvi. 吃草
- **英文释义**: land covered with grass where animals graze
- **词根词缀**: past(放牧)+ure(名词)→放牧地→牧场
- **例句**:
- The cattle were moved to a fresh pasture to ensure better nutrition.
- Farmers often rotate livestock among different pastures to prevent overgrazing and maintain soil health.
- **考频**: 8
## plain

- **音标**: plein
- **释义**: n. 平原, 草原, 朴实无华的东西, 无格式\na. 简单的, 明白的, 平常的, 不好看的, 朴素的, 清晰的, 普通的, 平坦的, 十足的\nadv. 清楚地, 显然地\n[计] 无格式
- **英文释义**: A large area of flat or gently rolling land; also used as an adjective meaning simple, clear, unadorned, or ordinary.
- **词根词缀**: plain(词根:平坦)→平坦的
- **例句**:
- The pioneers traveled westward across the vast Great Plains.
- Her plain speaking and straightforward reasoning impressed the committee.
- **考频**: 43
## plaintiff

- **音标**: 'pleintif
- **释义**: n. 原告, 起诉人\n[经] 原告, 原起诉人
- **英文释义**: The party who initiates a lawsuit in a court of law.
- **词根词缀**: plaintiff
- **例句**:
- The plaintiff filed a civil suit against the company for breach of contract.
- In this defamation case, the plaintiff must prove that the statement caused actual harm to their reputation.
## plaster

- **音标**: 'plæstә
- **释义**: n. 石膏, 灰泥, 安慰(缓解)用品, 膏药\nvt. 涂以灰泥, 敷以膏药, 掩饰, 使平服, 彻底击败, 减轻, 粘贴
- **英文释义**: A mixture of lime, sand, and water applied to walls and ceilings to form a smooth surface; also, a medicated adhesive bandage or paste used for treating wounds or pain.
- **词根词缀**: plaster(词根:涂抹)→石膏
- **例句**:
- The restoration team carefully removed the old plaster from the Victorian ceiling before applying a new lime-based plaster.
- After the minor burn, she applied a cooling aloe vera plaster to soothe the skin and reduce inflammation.
- **考频**: 1
