# 易混淆 (go- 开头的词)

> **类型**: 易混淆

> **学习提示**: government 政府，governor 州长/总督，gown 长袍/睡衣，gossip 闲聊/流言。

**4 词**

---

## government

- **音标**: 'gʌvәnmәnt
- **释义**: n. 政府, 内阁\n[经] 政府, 政治, 政体
- **英文释义**: the group of people who govern a country or state
- **词根词缀**: govern(统治)+ment(名词)→政府
- **例句**:
- The government has announced new policies to stimulate economic growth.
- Critics argue that the current government lacks transparency in its decision-making process.
- **考频**: 890
## governor

- **音标**: 'gʌvәnә
- **释义**: n. (美)州长, (英)总督, 统治者, 管理者, 理事\n[医] 节制器, 调节器
- **英文释义**: an official who governs a state (in the US) or a colony/territory (in the UK), or a person who manages or controls an organization or system
- **词根词缀**: govern(统治)+or(人)→统治者
- **例句**:
- The governor of California signed the new environmental bill into law.
- As a member of the university's board of governors, she played a key role in shaping academic policy.
- **考频**: 34
## gown

- **音标**: gaun
- **释义**: n. 睡衣, 法衣, 大学全体师生\nvt. 使穿睡衣
- **英文释义**: A long, loose garment worn for formal occasions, in academic or legal settings, or as sleepwear.
- **词根词缀**: gown→长袍
- **例句**:
- She wore a black academic gown during the commencement ceremony.
- The judge adjusted her scarlet robe and gown before delivering the verdict.
- **考频**: 2
## gossip

- **音标**: 'gɒsip
- **释义**: n. 闲聊, 随笔, 流言, 爱讲闲话的人\nvi. 说闲话, 闲聊
- **英文释义**: casual or unofficial talk about other people's personal lives, often involving unverified information
- **词根词缀**: gossip→闲聊
- **例句**:
- She dismissed the article as mere gossip with no factual basis.
- It is unprofessional for journalists to prioritize sensational gossip over substantive reporting.
- **考频**: 11
