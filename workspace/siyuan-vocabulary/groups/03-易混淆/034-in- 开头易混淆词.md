# in- 开头易混淆词

> **类型**: 易混淆

> **学习提示**: intimate (亲密的/暗示) vs intimidate (恐吓)；invalid (无效的/病人) vs invaluable (无价的/极宝贵的)。

**7 词**

---

## intimate

- **音标**: 'intimәt
- **释义**: a. 亲密的, 私人的, 秘密的\nvt. 暗示, 通知, 告诉\nn. 至交
- **英文释义**: closely acquainted or familiar; private or personal; to suggest or communicate indirectly
- **词根词缀**: in(不)+tim(害怕)+ate(形容词)→不害怕的→亲密的
- **例句**:
- Their intimate friendship, forged during years of collaborative research, made the academic conference feel like a family reunion.
- The report intimates that regulatory changes may be imminent, though no official announcement has been made.
- **考频**: 24
## intimidate

- **音标**: in'timideit
- **释义**: vt. 威胁, 恐吓\n[法] 恐吓, 威胁, 使恐惧
- **英文释义**: to make someone feel afraid or less confident, often to force them to do something
- **词根词缀**: in(使)+tim(害怕)+id+ate(动词)→使害怕→恐吓
- **例句**:
- The defendant claimed he was intimidated into confessing by aggressive questioning.
- Corporate executives sometimes use complex jargon to intimidate junior staff during meetings.
- **考频**: 11
## intricate

- **音标**: 'intrikit
- **释义**: a. 复杂的, 错综的, 缠结的, 难懂的
- **英文释义**: Having many complexly arranged or interconnected parts; difficult to understand or analyze
- **词根词缀**: in(进入)+tric(琐碎)+ate(形容词)→进入琐碎→复杂的
- **例句**:
- The novel's intricate plot involves multiple timelines and unreliable narrators.
- Scientists are still unraveling the intricate mechanisms underlying neural plasticity.
- **考频**: 13
## intrigue

- **音标**: in'tri:g
- **释义**: n. 阴谋, 复杂的事\nvi. 密谋, 私通\nvt. 激起...的兴趣, 用诡计取得
- **英文释义**: n. a secret or underhanded plan; v. to arouse curiosity or interest; to plot secretly
- **词根词缀**: in(进入)+trig(琐碎)+ue→进入琐碎→引起兴趣
- **例句**:
- The political intrigue surrounding the minister's resignation remains unresolved.
- Her unconventional research methodology intrigued the panel of examiners.
- **考频**: 7
## intrude

- **音标**: in'tru:d
- **释义**: vi. 闯入, 侵入\nvt. 强加于
- **英文释义**: to enter a place or situation without permission or invitation; to impose oneself or one's ideas on others in an unwelcome way
- **词根词缀**: in(进入)+trud(推)+e→推入→闯入
- **例句**:
- The reporter was accused of intruding on the family's private grief during the funeral.
- She felt uncomfortable when her colleague intruded his political opinions into the team meeting.
- **考频**: 7
## invalid

- **音标**: in'vælid
- **释义**: n. 病人, 残废者\na. 有病的, 无效的
- **英文释义**: n. a person who is chronically ill or disabled; a. not valid, legally or logically unsound
- **词根词缀**: in(不)+val(价值)+id(形容词)→无价值的→无效的
- **例句**:
- The hospital provides specialized care for the chronically ill and long-term invalids.
- His argument was dismissed by the court as invalid due to lack of supporting evidence.
- **考频**: 10
## invaluable

- **音标**: in'væljuәbl
- **释义**: a. 无价的, 价值无法衡量的
- **英文释义**: so valuable that its worth cannot be measured or estimated
- **词根词缀**: in(不)+valu(价值)+able(能)→无法估价的→无价的
- **例句**:
- Her mentor's guidance proved invaluable during the thesis writing process.
- The archival documents were an invaluable resource for historians studying the Cold War.
- **考频**: 6
