## faith

- **音标**: feiθ
- **释义**: n. 信心, 信任, 忠实, 保证\n[法] 信任, 信仰, 信念
- **英文释义**: strong belief or trust in someone or something, especially without proof
- **词根词缀**: faith(信任)→信任
- **例句**:
- Her unwavering faith in the scientific method guided her research through repeated failures.
- The judge emphasized that justice must be administered with impartiality and faith in due process.
- **考频**: 44
## faithful

- **音标**: 'feiθful
- **释义**: a. 忠实的, 详确的, 可靠的\nn. 信徒
- **英文释义**: loyal and steadfast in one's duties, beliefs, or relationships; accurate and true to the original
- **词根词缀**: faith(信任)+ful(充满)→充满信任的→忠诚的
- **例句**:
- She remained faithful to her principles despite intense pressure to compromise.
- The translation is remarkably faithful to the tone and nuance of the original Chinese text.
- **考频**: 11
## fame

- **音标**: feim
- **释义**: n. 名望, 名声, 传说
- **英文释义**: the state of being known or recognized by many people, especially for notable achievements
- **词根词缀**: fam(名声)+e→名声
- **例句**:
- Her groundbreaking research brought her international fame.
- He achieved literary fame with the publication of his first novel, which was widely acclaimed by critics.
- **考频**: 36
## famous

- **音标**: 'feimәs
- **释义**: a. 出名的, 极好的
- **英文释义**: well-known, especially by many people
- **词根词缀**: fam(名声)+ous(形容词)→著名的
- **例句**:
- The Great Wall is one of the most famous landmarks in the world.
- She became famous for her groundbreaking research in renewable energy.
- **考频**: 154
