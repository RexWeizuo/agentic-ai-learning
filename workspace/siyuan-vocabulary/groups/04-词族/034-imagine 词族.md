# imagine 词族

> **类型**: 词族

> **学习提示**: imaginary 指虚构的、假想的；imaginative 指富有想象力的。

## image

- **音标**: 'imidʒ
- **释义**: n. 影像, 肖像, 想象, 图像, 形象, 翻版\nvt. 作...的像, 反映, 想象, 象征\nn. 图像\n[计] 图象
- **英文释义**: a visual representation of something, such as a photograph, drawing, or mental picture; also refers to the public perception of a person or organization
- **词根词缀**: im(模仿)+age→模仿物→图像
- **例句**:
- The company launched a new advertising campaign to improve its public image.
- She closed her eyes and tried to form a clear mental image of the mountain landscape.
- **考频**: 220
## imaginary

- **音标**: i'mædʒinәri
- **释义**: a. 想像的, 虚构的, 假想的
- **英文释义**: existing only in the imagination; not real or factual
- **词根词缀**: imagin(想象)+ary→想象的
- **例句**:
- The 'imaginary friend' many children invent is a common psychological phenomenon studied in developmental psychology.
- In complex number theory, the term 'i' represents the imaginary unit, defined as the square root of -1.
- **考频**: 11
## imagination

- **音标**: i.mædʒi'neiʃәn
- **释义**: n. 想像, 听觉, 想像力\n[医] 想像
- **英文释义**: the ability to form new ideas, mental images, or concepts not present to the senses
- **词根词缀**: imagin(想象)+ation(名词)→想象力
- **例句**:
- Her vivid imagination enabled her to craft a compelling narrative for the thesis proposal.
- Scientific breakthroughs often stem from bold imagination combined with rigorous empirical testing.
- **考频**: 76
## imaginative

- **音标**: i'mædʒinәtiv
- **释义**: a. 想像的, 虚构的
- **英文释义**: having or showing creativity and the ability to think of new and original ideas
- **词根词缀**: imagin(想象)+ative→富有想象力的
- **例句**:
- Her imaginative approach to problem-solving impressed the entire research team.
- The novel's imaginative world-building reflects the author's deep literary craftsmanship.
- **考频**: 18
