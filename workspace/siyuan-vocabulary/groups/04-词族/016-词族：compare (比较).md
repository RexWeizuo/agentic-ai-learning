# 词族：compare (比较)

> **类型**: 词族

> **学习提示**: compare 动词，comparison 名词，comparative 指“比较的、相对的”，comparable 指“可比较的、比得上的”。

**4 词**

---

## comparable

- **音标**: 'kɒmpәrәbl
- **释义**: a. 可比较的, 比得上的
- **英文释义**: able to be compared because of similarities in nature, quality, or quantity
- **词根词缀**: com(一起)+par(比较)+able(能)→能比较的→可比较的
- **例句**:
- The new smartphone's battery life is comparable to that of its top competitors.
- Her research findings are not comparable with those from large-scale clinical trials due to the small sample size.
- **考频**: 32
## comparative

- **音标**: kәm'pærәtiv
- **释义**: a. 比较的, 相对的\nn. 对手
- **英文释义**: relating to or involving comparison; indicating a degree that is higher than one thing but not the highest
- **词根词缀**: com(一起)+par(比较)+ative(形容词)→比较的
- **例句**:
- The comparative advantage of this technology lies in its energy efficiency.
- In comparative linguistics, scholars analyze structural similarities and differences across languages.
- **考频**: 17
## compare

- **音标**: kәm'pєә
- **释义**: vt. 比较, 比喻, 对照\nvi. 相比\nn. 比较\n[计] 比较
- **英文释义**: to examine two or more things to identify their similarities and differences
- **词根词缀**: com(一起)+par(比较)→比较
- **例句**:
- The study compares the economic growth rates of ten developing countries over the past decade.
- No modern smartphone can compare with the latest model in terms of processing speed and battery life.
- **考频**: 289
## comparison

- **音标**: kәm'pærisn
- **释义**: n. 比较, 对照, 比喻\n[经] 比较, 对比
- **英文释义**: The act of examining two or more things to identify their similarities and differences
- **词根词缀**: com(一起)+par(比较)+ison(名词)→比较
- **例句**:
- A detailed comparison of the economic policies of the two countries revealed significant divergences in fiscal strategy.
- In academic writing, it is essential to make a fair and evidence-based comparison rather than relying on superficial analogies.
- **考频**: 64
