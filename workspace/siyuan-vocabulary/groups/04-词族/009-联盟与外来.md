# 联盟与外来

> **类型**: 词族

> **学习提示**: alien（外来的/外星人）派生 alienate（疏远）；align/alliance/ally 为联盟/结盟词族。

**5 词**

---

## alien

- **音标**: 'eiljәn
- **词性**: ['n.', 'adj.']
- **释义**: ==n. 外国人, 外侨\na. 外国的, 相异的==
- **英文释义**: v. transfer property or ownership\ns. not contained in or deriving from the essential nature of something\ns. being or from or characteristic of another place or part of the world
- **同义词**: foreign, strange
- **反义词**: native, familiar
- **词根词缀**: ali(其他)+en→其他地方的→外国的
- **搭配**: illegal alien, alien registration, alien culture
- **例句**:
- She applied for permanent residency as a legal alien in the United States.
- His ideas were so radical that they seemed alien to most members of the committee.
- **考频**: 24
## alienate

- **音标**: 'eiljәneit
- **词性**: v.t.
- **释义**: ==vt. 使疏远, 离间, 转让\n[法] 转让, 让与, 疏远==
- **英文释义**: v. make withdrawn or isolated or emotionally dissociated
- **同义词**: estranged, distance, disaffect
- **反义词**: reconcile, endear
- **词根词缀**: alien(外国的，相异的)+-ate(使…化)→使变得陌生/相异→使疏远；在法律语境中指使权利/财产脱离原主→转让
- **搭配**: alienate support, alienate someone from something, alienate customers
- **例句**:
- His harsh criticism alienated many of his longtime supporters.
- The company's environmental violations alienated local communities and damaged its reputation.
- **考频**: 7
## align

- **音标**: ә'lain
- **词性**: v.
- **释义**: ==vi. 排列, 排成一行, 结盟\nvt. 使结盟, 使成一行, 校正==
- **英文释义**: v. place in a line or arrange so as to be parallel or straight\nv. be or come into adjustment with\nv. align oneself with a group or a way of thinking\nv. bring (components or parts) into proper or desirable coordination correlation
- **同义词**: line up, coordinate, associate
- **反义词**: misalign, disorder
- **词根词缀**: ad-(to, toward)+linea(Latin: 'line')→'to bring into line'→arrange in a line or in agreement; adjust for consistency or cooperation
- **搭配**: align with, align to, align oneself with
- **例句**:
- The planets align once every few decades, creating a rare astronomical event.
- We need to align our marketing strategy with the company's core values.
- **考频**: 8
## alliance

- **音标**: ә'laiәns
- **释义**: ==n. 联盟, 联合\n[法] 同盟, 联盟, 联姻==
- **英文释义**: A formal agreement or partnership between countries, organizations, or individuals for mutual benefit or shared goals.
- **词根词缀**: al(to)+li(bind)+ance(noun)→binding together→联盟
- **例句**:
- The NATO alliance was established to ensure collective defense among its member states.
- A strategic alliance between the two tech companies enabled them to co-develop cutting-edge AI infrastructure.
- **考频**: 37
## ally

- **音标**: 'ælai. ә'lai
- **释义**: ==n. 同盟者, 同盟国, 助手\nvt. 使联盟, 使联合, 使有关系\nvi. 结盟==
- **英文释义**: a person or group that cooperates with another for a common purpose, especially in politics or war
- **同义词**: partner, associate
- **反义词**: enemy
- **词根词缀**: al(加强)+ly(绑)→绑在一起→同盟
- **例句**:
- The two countries formally allied themselves against a shared threat.
- She found an unlikely ally in her former rival when they joined forces to reform the outdated policy.
- **考频**: 16
