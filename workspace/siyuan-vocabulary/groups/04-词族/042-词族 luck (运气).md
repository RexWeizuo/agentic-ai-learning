# 词族 luck (运气)

> **类型**: 词族

> **学习提示**: 运气、彩票及诱惑（lure 常与运气/风险语境相关）

**4 词**

---

## luck

- **音标**: lʌk
- **释义**: n. 运气, 幸运, 好运, 侥幸\nvi. 靠好运成功
- **英文释义**: the force that brings good or bad things to someone by chance
- **词根词缀**: luck(运气)→运气
- **例句**:
- Her promotion was less due to competence and more to sheer luck.
- He didn’t prepare for the exam at all, yet he passed — pure luck.
- **考频**: 48
## lucky

- **音标**: 'lʌki
- **释义**: a. 幸运的, 吉祥的, 好运的, 侥幸的
- **英文释义**: having good luck; resulting from chance rather than skill or effort
- **词根词缀**: luck(运气)+y(形容词)→幸运的
- **例句**:
- She got the job—she was lucky to be interviewed on the same day the top candidate withdrew.
- It was lucky that the flight was delayed, as it allowed him to catch the crucial meeting he would otherwise have missed.
- **考频**: 56
## lottery

- **音标**: 'lɒtәri
- **释义**: n. 奖券, 彩票, 运气\n[经] 抽彩给奖法
- **英文释义**: A game of chance in which participants buy tickets for a chance to win prizes, often organized by governments or authorized bodies.
- **词根词缀**: lot(抽签)+ery(名词)→彩票
- **例句**:
- Winning the national lottery is largely a matter of pure luck, not skill.
- The government introduced a new lottery system to fund public education projects.
- **考频**: 10
## lure

- **音标**: luә
- **释义**: n. 饵, 诱惑\nvt. 引诱, 诱惑
- **英文释义**: something that attracts or tempts a person or animal, often by offering pleasure or advantage
- **词根词缀**: lure→诱惑
- **例句**:
- The company used high salaries and flexible working hours as a lure to attract top talent.
- Critics warned that the promise of quick wealth was merely a lure to trap inexperienced investors.
- **考频**: 20
