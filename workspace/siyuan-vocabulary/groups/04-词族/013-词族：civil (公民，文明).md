# 词族：civil (公民，文明)

> **类型**: 词族

> **学习提示**: civil 派生词族，注意 civilian 指“平民”，与军人相对。

**4 词**

---

## civil

- **音标**: 'sivәl
- **释义**: a. 市民的, 公民的, 有礼貌的\n[法] 公民的, 国民的, 民用的
- **英文释义**: relating to ordinary citizens or civil society, not military or religious; polite and respectful in behavior
- **词根词缀**: civ(公民)+il(形容词)→公民的→文明的
- **例句**:
- The court handled the dispute under civil law rather than criminal law.
- Despite their disagreement, both candidates maintained a civil tone throughout the debate.
- **考频**: 55
## civilian

- **音标**: si'viljәn
- **释义**: n. 平民, 民法专家\na. 平民的, 百姓的, 民用的
- **英文释义**: A person who is not a member of the armed forces or police force; a non-military, non-official individual.
- **词根词缀**: civ(公民)+il(形容词)+ian(人)→公民→平民
- **例句**:
- The bombing killed dozens of civilians, including women and children.
- Civilian authorities urged restraint after the military declared a state of emergency.
- **考频**: 23
## civilisation

- **音标**: ,sivilai'zeiʃən;-li'z-
- **释义**: n. 文明, 文明世界, 文化, 开化, 教化
- **英文释义**: the stage of human social development and organization that is considered most advanced, characterized by complex social structures, culture, science, and governance
- **词根词缀**: civ(公民)+il(形容词)+is(使)+ation(名词)→使文明化→文明
- **例句**:
- The ancient Egyptians built one of the world's earliest and most sophisticated civilizations.
- Globalization has intensified interactions among different civilizations, fostering both cooperation and cultural tension.
- **考频**: 4
## civilise

- **音标**: 'sivilaiz
- **释义**: vt. 开化, 使文明, (非正式)教化, 使文雅, 教育, 教导\nvi. 变成文明社会
- **英文释义**: to make a society, group, or person more civilized, especially by introducing education, laws, and moral values
- **词根词缀**: civ(citizen)+il+-ise(使…化/…化)
- **例句**:
- Colonial powers often claimed they were civilising indigenous peoples, though such efforts were frequently exploitative and culturally destructive.
- Education systems play a crucial role in civilising future generations by fostering critical thinking, empathy, and civic responsibility.
