# employ 词族

> **类型**: 词族

> **学习提示**: 雇用相关词汇，注意 -er 和 -ee 后缀的区别

**4 词**

---

## employ

- **音标**: im'plɒi
- **释义**: n. 雇用\nvt. 雇用, 使用, 使从事于
- **英文释义**: to hire someone for work; to use something for a particular purpose
- **词根词缀**: em(进入)+ploy(折叠)→卷入→雇佣
- **例句**:
- The company decided to employ five new engineers to strengthen its R&D team.
- Effective time management employs prioritization and disciplined scheduling to maximize productivity.
- **考频**: 143
## employee

- **音标**: .emplɒi'i:
- **释义**: n. 职员, 员工, 受雇人员\n[化] 职工; 雇员
- **英文释义**: a person who works for an employer for wages or salary
- **词根词缀**: employ(雇佣)+ee(被…的人)→被雇佣的人→雇员
- **例句**:
- All employees are required to attend the annual compliance training.
- The company announced a 5% salary increase for full-time employees effective next month.
- **考频**: 395
## employer

- **音标**: im'plɒiә
- **释义**: n. 雇主, 老板\n[经] 雇主, 业主
- **英文释义**: A person or organization that hires and pays people to work for them.
- **词根词缀**: employ(雇佣)+er(做…的人)→雇佣的人→雇主
- **例句**:
- The employer is legally required to provide a safe working environment.
- Graduates should carefully evaluate potential employers based on corporate culture and career development opportunities.
- **考频**: 191
## employment

- **音标**: im'plɒimәnt
- **释义**: n. 雇用, 职业, 工作\n[经] 职业, 雇用, 职工招请
- **英文释义**: the state of having a paid job or the process of hiring people for work
- **词根词缀**: employ(雇佣)+ment(名词)→雇佣→就业
- **例句**:
- Full employment is a key goal of macroeconomic policy.
- The company announced a new round of employment to expand its research division.
- **考频**: 130
