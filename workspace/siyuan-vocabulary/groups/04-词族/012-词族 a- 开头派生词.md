## attend

- **音标**: ә'tend
- **释义**: ==vt. 参加, 照料, 伴随\nvi. 专心于, 照顾, 服侍, 出席==
- **英文释义**: v. be present at (meetings, church services, university), etc.\nv. take charge of or deal with\nv. to accompany as a circumstance or follow as a result\nv. give heed (to)
- **同义词**: participate, be present
- **反义词**: absent
- **词根词缀**: at(加强)+tend(伸展)→伸展到→参加
- **搭配**: attend to, attend a meeting
- **例句**:
- She attends the same school.
- The nurse attended to the patient.
- **考频**: 226
## attendant

- **音标**: ә'tendәnt
- **释义**: ==n. 随从, 侍者, 出席者\na. 侍候的, 伴随的, 出席的, 在场的==
- **英文释义**: A person who serves, accompanies, or is present at an event; often in a supportive or auxiliary role.
- **词根词缀**: at(to)+tend(stretch)+ant(person)→one who stretches to→服务员
- **例句**:
- The flight attendant provided passengers with safety instructions before takeoff.
- As an attendant at the academic conference, she assisted speakers with their presentation materials and managed registration.
- **考频**: 10
## attention

- **音标**: ә'tenʃәn
- **释义**: ==n. 注意, 注意力\n[计] 引起注意信号==
- **英文释义**: the act of focusing one's mind on something
- **同义词**: focus, notice
- **反义词**: neglect
- **词根词缀**: attent(注意)+ion→注意
- **搭配**: pay attention to, draw attention to
- **例句**:
- The professor emphasized that close attention to detail is essential for success in academic writing.
- Her groundbreaking research has attracted widespread attention from scholars across the globe.
- **考频**: 445
## attract

- **音标**: ә'trækt
- **释义**: ==vt. 吸引, 诱惑\nvi. 有吸引力==
- **英文释义**: v. direct toward itself or oneself by means of some psychological power or physical attributes\nv. be attractive to\nv. exert a force on (a body) causing it to approach or prevent it from moving away
- **同义词**: draw, appeal
- **反义词**: repel
- **词根词缀**: at(朝向)+tract(拉)→拉向→吸引
- **例句**:
- The museum attracts millions of visitors.
- Her charm attracted everyone.
- **考频**: 217
## attractive

- **音标**: ә'træktiv
- **释义**: ==a. 吸引人的, 有魅力的\n[法] 有吸引力的, 有迷惑力的==
- **英文释义**: pleasing or appealing to the senses or mind, especially in a way that causes interest or desire
- **词根词缀**: at(to)+tract(draw)+ive(adjective)→drawing to→有吸引力的
- **例句**:
- The company offered an attractive salary package to recruit top talent.
- She presented a well-structured and attractive argument that persuaded the committee.
- **考频**: 122
## avail

- **音标**: /əˈveɪl/
- **词性**: v./n.
- **释义**: ==利用；效用，利益==
- **英文释义**: to help or be useful; benefit or advantage
- **同义词**: benefit, use, profit, utilize
- **反义词**: disadvantage, harm
- **词根词缀**: a(加强)+vail(价值)→有价值→效用
- **搭配**: avail oneself of, of no avail, to little avail
- **例句**:
- He availed himself of the opportunity.
- All their efforts were of no avail.
- **考频**: 3
## available

- **音标**: ә'veilәbl
- **释义**: ==a. 可利用的, 可获得的, 有效的\n[医] 有效的, 可得的==
- **英文释义**: able to be used, obtained, or accessed
- **同义词**: accessible, obtainable
- **反义词**: unavailable
- **词根词缀**: avail(效用)+able→可用的
- **搭配**: available for, readily available
- **例句**:
- The research data is not yet available to the public.
- Only candidates with relevant experience will be considered; qualified applicants are currently available in limited numbers.
- **考频**: 284
