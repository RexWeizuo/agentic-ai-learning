# ent- 娱乐/热情

## entertain

- **音标**: .entә'tein
- **释义**: vt. 娱乐, 招待, 怀抱\nvi. 款待
- **英文释义**: to provide amusement or enjoyment; to host or treat someone socially; to consider or hold (an idea) in one's mind
- **词根词缀**: enter(进入)+tain(保持)→保持进入→娱乐
- **例句**:
- The comedian's sharp wit helped entertain the audience for over two hours.
- Many students find it difficult to entertain the possibility that their long-held assumptions might be flawed.
- **考频**: 57
## entertainment

- **音标**: .entә'teinmәnt
- **释义**: n. 娱乐, 款待, 娱乐表演
- **英文释义**: the activity of providing or enjoying amusement or pleasure
- **词根词缀**: enter(进入)+tain(保持)+ment(名词)→娱乐
- **例句**:
- The film festival offers a wide range of entertainment, from avant-garde documentaries to mainstream comedies.
- While tourism boosts the local economy, it also raises concerns about the commercialization of cultural entertainment.
- **考频**: 74
## enthusiasm

- **音标**: in'θju:ziæzәm
- **释义**: n. 巨大的热情, 热心
- **英文释义**: a strong feeling of interest and excitement about something
- **词根词缀**: en(使)+thus(神)+iasm(名词)→被神附身→热情
- **例句**:
- Her enthusiasm for sustainable development inspired the entire team.
- The candidate's enthusiasm for public service was evident in both her policy proposals and campaign speeches.
- **考频**: 38
## enthusiastic

- **音标**: in.θju:zi'æstik
- **释义**: a. 狂热的, 热心的, 热烈的
- **英文释义**: having or showing intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval
- **词根词缀**: en(使)+thus(神)+iastic(形容词)→热情的
- **例句**:
- The students were enthusiastic about the new interdisciplinary curriculum.
- She gave an enthusiastic endorsement of the policy reform during the parliamentary debate.
- **考频**: 30
