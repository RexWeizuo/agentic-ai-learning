# sur- 前缀 (超过/在上面)

> **类型**: 词根

> **学习提示**: sur- 是 super- 的变体，表示“超过、在上面”。surveillance (监视) 和 surplus (过剩) 是考研重点。

**8 词**

---

## surpass

- **音标**: sә'pɑ:s
- **释义**: vt. 超越, 凌驾, 胜过
- **英文释义**: to exceed or go beyond in quality, quantity, or degree
- **词根词缀**: sur(超过)+pass(通过)→超过→超越
- **例句**:
- Her academic performance surpassed all expectations, earning her a full scholarship to Oxford.
- The latest AI model surpasses its predecessors in both accuracy and computational efficiency.
- **考频**: 18
## surplus

- **音标**: 'sә:plәs
- **释义**: n. 剩余, 过剩, 盈余\na. 过剩的, 剩余的
- **英文释义**: An amount that is more than what is needed, used, or required; especially the excess of income over expenditure or of supply over demand.
- **词根词缀**: sur(超过)+plus(加)→超过加→剩余
- **例句**:
- The government reported a budget surplus of $12 billion for the fiscal year.
- A persistent trade surplus may lead to tensions with trading partners due to concerns about unfair competition.
- **考频**: 26
## surprise

- **音标**: sә'praiz
- **释义**: n. 惊奇, 奇袭, 诧异\nvt. 使惊奇, 撞见, 奇袭
- **英文释义**: a feeling of mild astonishment or shock caused by something unexpected; or an unexpected attack or action
- **词根词缀**: sur(超过)+prise(拿)→超出预期地拿→使惊奇
- **例句**:
- Her promotion came as a complete surprise to everyone in the department.
- The army launched a surprise attack at dawn, catching the enemy off guard.
- **考频**: 72
## surrender

- **音标**: sә'rendә
- **释义**: vt. 交出, 放弃, 使投降, 让与\nvi. 投降, 自首\nn. 交出, 放弃, 投降
- **英文释义**: to give up control, possession, or resistance, often under pressure or after defeat
- **词根词缀**: sur(向上)+render(给予)→向上给予→投降
- **例句**:
- The besieged army refused to surrender despite dwindling supplies.
- She finally surrendered her claim to the inheritance after years of legal dispute.
- **考频**: 9
## surveillance

- **音标**: sә:'veilәns
- **释义**: n. 监视, 监督\n[电] 侦测
- **英文释义**: the close observation of a person, place, or activity, especially by a government or organization for security or law enforcement purposes
- **词根词缀**: sur(在上面)+veill(看)+ance(名词)→在上面看→监视
- **例句**:
- The police conducted covert surveillance on the suspect's apartment for three weeks.
- Mass surveillance by digital platforms raises serious concerns about privacy and civil liberties in modern society.
- **考频**: 8
## survey

- **音标**: sә'vei
- **释义**: n. 纵览, 视察, 测量, 俯瞰, 调查\nvt. 审视, 视察, 俯瞰, 通盘考虑\nvi. 测量土地
- **英文释义**: A comprehensive examination or investigation of a subject, area, or group; or the act of measuring land or assessing features from a broad perspective.
- **词根词缀**: sur(在上面)+vey(看)→在上面看→调查
- **例句**:
- The government conducted a nationwide survey to assess public opinion on climate policy.
- Before constructing the new highway, engineers had to survey the terrain for elevation changes and geological hazards.
- **考频**: 271
## survive

- **音标**: sә'vaiv
- **释义**: vt. 比...活得长, 生存, 生还, 幸免于\nvi. 活下来, 幸存
- **英文释义**: to continue to live or exist, especially after a dangerous, difficult, or life-threatening situation
- **词根词缀**: sur(超过)+viv(活)+e→超过活着→幸存
- **例句**:
- Many species failed to survive the drastic climate change during the Ice Age.
- She survived the plane crash but spent six months recovering in hospital.
- **考频**: 161
