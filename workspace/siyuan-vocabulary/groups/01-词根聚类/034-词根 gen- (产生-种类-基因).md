# 词根 gen- (产生/种类/基因)

> **类型**: 词根

> **学习提示**: gen- 表示“出生、产生、种类”。gene/genetic 与基因相关，generate 是产生，genius 原指守护神/天赋。

**7 词**

---

## gene

- **音标**: dʒi:n
- **释义**: n. 基因\n[化] 基因(遗传因子)
- **英文释义**: A unit of heredity that is transferred from a parent to offspring and determines some characteristic of the offspring.
- **词根词缀**: gen(产生)→基因
- **例句**:
- Scientists have identified a gene associated with increased risk of Alzheimer's disease.
- The CRISPR-Cas9 system allows precise editing of a specific gene to correct genetic disorders.
- **考频**: 96
## generate

- **音标**: 'dʒenәreit
- **释义**: vt. 产生, 发生, 导致\n[电] 产生
- **英文释义**: to produce or create something, especially energy, data, or a reaction
- **词根词缀**: gen(产生)+er+ate(动词)→产生
- **例句**:
- The new policy is expected to generate significant public debate.
- Solar panels generate electricity by converting sunlight into energy.
- **考频**: 119
## generator

- **音标**: 'dʒenәreitә
- **释义**: n. 产生器, 发电机, 生产者\n[计] 发生器
- **英文释义**: A device or system that produces electricity, data, or other outputs; in computing, a program or function that generates code, values, or content.
- **词根词缀**: gen(产生)+er+ator(名词)→产生者→发电机
- **例句**:
- The power plant uses a turbine-driven generator to convert mechanical energy into electrical energy.
- In Python, a generator function yields a sequence of values lazily, making it memory-efficient for large datasets.
- **考频**: 3
## genetic

- **音标**: dʒi'netik
- **释义**: a. 创始的, 遗传的, 起源的, 遗传学的\n[医] 生殖的, 遗传的
- **英文释义**: relating to genes, heredity, or the origin and development of organisms
- **词根词缀**: gen(产生)+et+ic(形容词)→基因的
- **例句**:
- Mutations in genetic material can lead to inherited diseases.
- Recent advances in genetic engineering have raised profound ethical questions in medical research.
- **考频**: 114
## genius

- **音标**: 'dʒi:njәs
- **释义**: n. 天才, 天赋, 精神, 神魔\n[医] 特征
- **英文释义**: A person who has exceptional intellectual or creative ability; also, an innate talent or natural aptitude for a particular activity.
- **词根词缀**: gen(产生)+ius→天生的→天才
- **例句**:
- Einstein's genius in theoretical physics revolutionized our understanding of space and time.
- Her genius for language acquisition enabled her to master six languages before the age of twenty.
- **考频**: 50
## gender

- **音标**: 'dʒendә
- **释义**: n. 性\nvt. 产生
- **英文释义**: the social and cultural roles, behaviors, and identities associated with being male, female, or non-binary; (rarely) to assign or categorize by gender
- **词根词缀**: gen(产生)+der→种类→性别
- **例句**:
- Gender norms vary significantly across cultures and historical periods.
- Recent studies have begun to gender the analysis of leadership styles, highlighting how assumptions about masculinity influence organizational expectations.
- **考频**: 133
## generalise

- **音标**: 'dʒenәrәlaiz
- **释义**: vt.vi. (使)一般化, 从...引出一般性结论, 泛论, 概括出, 归纳出, 使广义化
- **英文释义**: to form a general principle or conclusion from specific instances; to apply something broadly without sufficient evidence
- **词根词缀**: gen(产生)+er+al(形容词)+ise(动词)→使普遍→概括
- **例句**:
- Researchers must avoid generalising from a small, unrepresentative sample.
- While the findings are compelling, it would be premature to generalise them to all socioeconomic groups.
- **考频**: 1
