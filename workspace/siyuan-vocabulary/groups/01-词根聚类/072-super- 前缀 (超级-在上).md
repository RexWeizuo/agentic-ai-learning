# super- 前缀 (超级/在上)

> **类型**: 词根

> **学习提示**: super- 表示“在...之上、超过、超级”。注意 superficial (表面的) 和 superfluous (多余的) 的区分。

**9 词**

---

## super

- **音标**: 'sju:pә
- **释义**: n. 跑龙套角色, 冗员, 特级品, 特大号, 管理人\na. 上等的, 特大的, 超级的, 极好的, 十分的, 过分的\nadv. 非常
- **英文释义**: extremely good or large; used informally to emphasize quality, size, or degree
- **词根词缀**: super(超级)→超级的
- **例句**:
- The new AI model is super efficient in processing natural language tasks.
- She was super confident during her presentation at the international conference.
- **考频**: 22
## superficial

- **音标**: .sju:pә'fiʃәl
- **释义**: n. 表面, 外表\na. 表面的, 肤浅的, 面积的
- **英文释义**: relating to the surface only; lacking depth or thoroughness
- **词根词缀**: super(在…之上)+fic(面)+ial(形容词)→表面的
- **例句**:
- His understanding of quantum physics was superficial, as he could only recall basic definitions without grasping underlying principles.
- The report provided a superficial analysis of the economic crisis, ignoring structural causes and long-term implications.
- **考频**: 22
## superfluous

- **音标**: sju'pә:fluәs
- **释义**: a. 多余的, 过剩的
- **英文释义**: Unnecessary, especially through being more than enough
- **词根词缀**: super(超过)+flu(流)+ous(形容词)→多余的
- **例句**:
- The report was filled with superfluous details that obscured its main argument.
- In academic writing, superfluous words should be eliminated to enhance clarity and concision.
- **考频**: 3
## superior

- **英文释义**: higher in rank, quality, or authority; outstanding or better than others

## superstition

- **音标**: .sju:pә'stiʃәn
- **释义**: n. 迷信\n[法] 迷信
- **英文释义**: An irrational belief that an object, action, or circumstance not logically related to a course of events influences its outcome.
- **词根词缀**: super(超过)+stit(站)+ion(名词)→站在事物之上→迷信
