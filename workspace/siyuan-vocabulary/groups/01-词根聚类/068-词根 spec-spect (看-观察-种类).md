# 词根 spec/spect (看/观察/种类)

> **类型**: 词根

> **学习提示**: 共享词根 spec/spect，表示“看”或“种类”，注意 spectacle(景象) 与 spectacular(壮观的) 的词性区别。

**6 词**

---

## spectacle

- **音标**: 'spektәkl
- **释义**: n. 引人羡慕的东西, 景象, 眼镜, 场面, 公开展示
- **英文释义**: a striking or impressive sight, event, or display
- **词根词缀**: spect(看)+acle(名词)→看的东西→景象
- **例句**:
- The Olympic opening ceremony was a dazzling spectacle of light, music, and cultural symbolism.
- Her academic achievements made her a spectacle of excellence among her peers.
- **考频**: 10
## spectacular

- **音标**: spek'tækjulә
- **释义**: a. 公开展示的, 惊人的, 壮观的\nn. 奇观, 惊人之举, 展览物
- **英文释义**: extremely impressive or dramatic, especially in appearance or effect
- **词根词缀**: spect(看)+acul(名词)+ar(形容词)→景象的→壮观的
- **例句**:
- The fireworks display was a spectacular celebration of the city's bicentennial.
- Her spectacular performance in the final debate secured her landslide victory.
- **考频**: 8
## spectator

- **音标**: spek'teitә
- **释义**: n. 观众, 目击者, 旁观者
- **英文释义**: a person who watches an event, performance, or activity without taking part in it
- **词根词缀**: spect(看)+ator(人)→看的人→观众
- **例句**:
- The stadium was packed with enthusiastic spectators cheering for their favorite team.
- As a mere spectator to the negotiation, she had no authority to influence the outcome.
- **考频**: 21
## spectrum

- **音标**: 'spektrәm
- **释义**: n. 光谱, 范围, 系列\n[化] 光谱
- **英文释义**: A range of related qualities, ideas, or phenomena, especially when considered as a continuum; in physics, the band of colors produced when light is dispersed by a prism.
- **词根词缀**: spect(看)+rum(名词)→看出的范围→光谱
- **例句**:
- The political views of the candidates span a wide spectrum, from far-left to conservative.
- Modern linguistics recognizes that language change occurs across a spectrum of social, regional, and stylistic variables.
- **考频**: 17
## speculate

- **音标**: 'spekjuleit
- **释义**: vi. 深思, 推测, 投机\n[经] 投机
- **英文释义**: to form a theory or conjecture about something without firm evidence; to invest in stocks, property, or commodities in the hope of gaining profits from price fluctuations
- **词根词缀**: spec(看)+ul(小)+ate(动词)→仔细看→推测
- **例句**:
- Economists speculate that inflation will ease in the next quarter.
- He lost a substantial amount when he speculated heavily on volatile tech stocks.
- **考频**: 26
## specimen

- **音标**: 'spesimәn
- **释义**: n. 样品, 标本, 试料\n[化] 试样
- **英文释义**: A small quantity or part of something, especially one that is taken to be examined, tested, or displayed as representative.
- **词根词缀**: spec(看)+imen(名词)→供看的东西→标本
- **例句**:
- The biologist collected a blood specimen from the patient for laboratory analysis.
- This fossil is an excellent specimen of a Jurassic marine reptile, widely cited in paleontological research.
- **考频**: 4
