# inter- 前缀 (交互/之间)

> **类型**: 词根

> **学习提示**: inter- 表示在...之间、相互。注意 interfere (干涉/妨碍) 与 intervene (介入/调停) 的语境差异。

**9 词**

---

## interact

- **音标**: .intәr'ækt
- **释义**: vi. 互相影响, 交互作用\nn. 幕间剧, 幕间休息
- **英文释义**: to act upon or influence each other
- **词根词缀**: inter(相互)+act(行动)→相互作用
- **例句**:
- Children learn social skills as they interact with peers in group activities.
- The drug may interact with certain antibiotics, leading to reduced efficacy or adverse effects.
- **考频**: 64
## intercourse

- **音标**: 'intәkɒ:s
- **释义**: n. 交往, 交流\n[医] 交际, 往来
- **英文释义**: communication or interaction between people, especially in social, intellectual, or cultural contexts
- **词根词缀**: inter(相互)+course(跑)→相互跑→交流
- **例句**:
- Regular intellectual intercourse with scholars from different disciplines enriched her research perspective.
- The treaty aims to promote economic intercourse and mutual understanding between the two nations.
- **考频**: 1
## interface

- **音标**: 'intәfeis
- **释义**: n. 分界面, 接触面, 界面\n[计] 接口, 界面
- **英文释义**: A surface or boundary where two different systems, substances, or components meet and interact; in computing, a shared boundary that enables communication between software or hardware components.
- **词根词缀**: inter(相互)+face(面)→相互的面→接口
- **例句**:
- The interface between the ocean and the atmosphere plays a critical role in climate regulation.
- A well-designed user interface significantly improves software usability and reduces learning time for new users.
- **考频**: 2
## interfere

- **音标**: .intә'fiә
- **释义**: vi. 妨碍, 冲突, 干涉, 抵触\n[医] 干扰, 干涉, 阻碍, 碰腿(马)
- **英文释义**: to get involved in a situation in a way that is not wanted or helpful; to hinder or obstruct a process or action
- **词根词缀**: inter(相互)+fer(带来)+e→相互带来→干涉
- **例句**:
- Excessive government regulation may interfere with market efficiency.
- The loud construction noise interfered with her concentration during the exam.
## interference

- **音标**: .intә'fiәrәns
- **释义**: n. 冲突, 干涉\n[计] 干扰
- **英文释义**: The action or process of interfering, especially in a way that hinders or disrupts an activity, signal, or system.
- **词根词缀**: inter(相互)+fer(带来)+ence(名词)→干涉
- **例句**:
- The experiment was invalidated due to electromagnetic interference from nearby equipment.
- Government interference in market mechanisms may lead to inefficiency and unintended economic consequences.
- **考频**: 43
## intermediate

- **音标**: .intә'mi:diәt
- **释义**: n. 中间物, 调停者, 中级\na. 中间的, 中级的\nvi. 起媒介作用\n[计] 中级
- **英文释义**: occurring or situated between two points, stages, or things; of medium difficulty or complexity
- **词根词缀**: inter(在…之间)+medi(中间)+ate(形容词)→中间的
- **例句**:
- The course is designed for intermediate learners who have mastered basic grammar but need to develop fluency.
- In diplomatic negotiations, a neutral country often serves as an intermediate to facilitate communication between conflicting parties.
- **考频**: 6
## interval

- **音标**: 'intәvәl
- **释义**: n. 间隔, 距离, 间歇, 间隙\n[计] 时间间隔
- **英文释义**: A period of time between two events or points; a space between two things.
- **词根词缀**: inter(在…之间)+val(墙)→墙之间的空间→间隔

## intervene

- **音标**: .intә'vi:n
- **释义**: vi. 插入, 调停, 干涉\n[经] 进场干预
- **英文释义**: to come between or become involved in a situation, especially to try to influence or change its course
- **词根词缀**: inter(在…之间)+ven(来)+e→来到中间→干预
- **例句**:
- The government decided to intervene in the labor dispute to prevent a nationwide strike.
- When the two countries were on the brink of war, a neutral third party intervened to mediate peace talks.
- **考频**: 21
