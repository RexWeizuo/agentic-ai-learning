# 词根：mon-/mono- (单一/提醒)

> **类型**: 词根

> **学习提示**: 包含 mono-(单一) 和 monere(提醒/警告) 两个同源词根，考研常合并记忆。

**6 词**

---

## monarch

- **音标**: 'mɒnәk
- **释义**: n. 帝王, 统治者, 元首\n[医] 单原型(植)
- **英文释义**: a sovereign head of state, especially a king, queen, or emperor
- **词根词缀**: mon(单一)+arch(统治)→单一统治者→君主
- **例句**:
- The British monarch plays a largely ceremonial role in modern constitutional governance.
- Historians debate the extent to which the absolutist monarch controlled both policy and administration in 17th-century France.
- **考频**: 12
## monetary

- **音标**: 'mʌnitәri
- **释义**: a. 货币的, 金钱的\n[经] 货币的, 金融的
- **英文释义**: Relating to money, especially as used in a country's economy or financial system.
- **词根词缀**: monet(钱)+ary(形容词)→钱的→货币的
- **例句**:
- The central bank adjusted its monetary policy to curb inflation.
- Monetary stability is essential for sustainable economic growth.
- **考频**: 36
## monopoly

- **音标**: mә'nɒpәli
- **释义**: n. 垄断, 专卖权, 独占事业\n[经] 垄断, 专利品, 垄断(权)独占
- **英文释义**: The exclusive control of a commodity or service in a particular market, often by one company or group.
- **词根词缀**: mono(单一)+poly(卖)→单一卖→垄断
- **例句**:
- The government broke up the telecommunications monopoly to encourage competition.
- Microsoft was accused of using its operating system monopoly to stifle innovation in web browsers.
- **考频**: 28
## monotonous

- **音标**: mә'nɒtәnәs
- **释义**: a. 单调的, 无变化的
- **英文释义**: boringly repetitive and lacking in variety
- **词根词缀**: mono(单一)+ton(声音)+ous(形容词)→单一声音→单调的
- **例句**:
- The monotonous drone of the lecturer's voice made it hard to stay focused during the three-hour seminar.
- Working on the assembly line proved monotonous, as each task was identical and performed every 47 seconds.
- **考频**: 3
## monster

- **音标**: 'mɒnstә
- **释义**: n. 怪物, 恶人, 巨物\n[医] 畸胎
- **英文释义**: a large, frightening, or unnatural creature; a cruel or evil person; or an unusually large or powerful thing
- **词根词缀**: monstr(展示)+er(物)→展示的东西→怪物
- **例句**:
- The ancient myth describes the monster as a hybrid of lion, goat, and serpent.
- His ruthless business tactics earned him the reputation of a corporate monster.
- **考频**: 10
## monument

- **音标**: 'mɒnjumәnt
- **释义**: n. 纪念碑, 纪念物, 石碑
- **英文释义**: A structure or object built to honor a person, event, or idea, often of historical or cultural significance.
- **词根词缀**: monu(提醒)+ment(名词)→提醒的东西→纪念碑
- **例句**:
- The Washington Monument stands as a towering tribute to the first U.S. president.
- Archaeologists discovered a well-preserved Roman monument that provided new insights into imperial propaganda.
- **考频**: 17
