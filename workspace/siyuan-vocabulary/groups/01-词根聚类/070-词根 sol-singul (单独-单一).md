# 词根 sol/singul (单独/单一)

> **类型**: 词根

> **学习提示**: 表示“单独、唯一”。sole(唯一的), solitary(独居的), solo(独奏), singular(单数的/非凡的)。

**4 词**

---

## sole

- **音标**: sәul
- **释义**: n. 脚掌, 鞋底, 底部\na. 唯一的, 仅有的, 单独的, 独身的\nvt. 上鞋底, 触底
- **英文释义**: n. the bottom surface of the foot or a shoe; a. being the only one of its kind; vt. to attach a sole to (a shoe)
- **词根词缀**: sole(词根)→唯一的
- **例句**:
- She felt a sharp pebble pressing into the sole of her running shoe.
- He is the sole survivor of the plane crash, making him the only witness to the incident.
- **考频**: 20
## solitary

- **音标**: 'sɒlitәri
- **释义**: n. 独居者\na. 孤独的, 独居的
- **英文释义**: existing or living alone; isolated from others
- **词根词缀**: sol(单独)+itary(形容词后缀)→孤独的
- **例句**:
- He spent years as a solitary in the remote mountains, seeking spiritual enlightenment.
- The solitary nature of academic research often requires intense focus and self-discipline.
- **考频**: 10
## solo

- **音标**: 'sәulәu
- **释义**: n. 独奏, 独唱, 单独表演\na. 单独的, 独奏的, 独唱的\nvi. 放单飞
- **英文释义**: A musical performance by a single performer, or an activity done alone without assistance.
- **词根词缀**: sol(单独)+o(名词后缀)→独奏
- **例句**:
- The jazz pianist delivered an impressive solo that lasted over five minutes.
- After months of rigorous training, she was finally ready to solo in her first flight test.
- **考频**: 3
## singular

- **音标**: 'siŋgjulә
- **释义**: n. 单数\na. 异常的, 单一的
- **英文释义**: 1. (n.) the grammatical number used for one person or thing; 2. (adj.) unusual or remarkable; existing as the only one of its kind
- **词根词缀**: singul(单一)+ar(形容词)→单一的
- **例句**:
- The verb must agree with the subject in person and singular or plural number.
- Her singular insight into economic policy earned her a place on the advisory committee.
- **考频**: 7
