# 词根：min- (小)

> **类型**: 词根

> **学习提示**: 共享词根 min-，表示“小、少、次要”。

**4 词**

---

## miniature

- **音标**: 'miniәtʃә
- **释义**: n. 缩图, 小画像\na. 小规模的, 纤小的
- **英文释义**: A very small version of something; a tiny, detailed portrait painting.
- **词根词缀**: mini(小)+ature(名词)→微型画
- **例句**:
- The museum displayed a 17th-century miniature painted on ivory.
- Urban farming is a miniature version of industrial agriculture, designed for limited spaces.
- **考频**: 7
## minimise

- **释义**: vt. 使减到最少/最小, 使降到最低限度, 使缩到最小, 极度轻视
- **英文释义**: to reduce something to the smallest possible amount, degree, or extent
- **词根词缀**: minim(最小)+ise(动词)→最小化
- **例句**:
- The company implemented new safety protocols to minimise the risk of workplace accidents.
- Researchers strive to minimise bias in their experimental design to ensure the validity of results.
- **考频**: 1
## minimum

- **音标**: 'minimәm
- **释义**: a. 最小的, 最低的\nn. 最小值\n[计] 最小值
- **英文释义**: the smallest or lowest possible amount, number, or level
- **词根词缀**: minim(最小)+um(名词)→最小值
- **例句**:
- The minimum wage in this country has been raised to $15 per hour.
- Candidates must achieve a minimum score of 7.0 on the IELTS to be eligible for admission.
- **考频**: 54
## minor

- **音标**: 'mainә
- **释义**: n. 未成年人, 副修科目\na. 较小的, 二流的, 未成年的\nvi. 副修\n[计] 次要
- **英文释义**: a person under the legal age of adulthood; a secondary field of study; not major or principal
- **词根词缀**: min(小)+or(比较级)→较小的
- **例句**:
- In most jurisdictions, a minor cannot enter into a binding contract without parental consent.
- She decided to minor in linguistics while pursuing her major in computer science.
- **考频**: 58
