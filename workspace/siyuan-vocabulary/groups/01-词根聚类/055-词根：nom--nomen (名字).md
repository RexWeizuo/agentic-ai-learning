# 词根：nom-/nomen (名字)

> **类型**: 词根

> **学习提示**: 共享词根 nom-/nomen，表示“名字、命名”。

**4 词**

---

## nominal

- **音标**: 'nɒminl
- **释义**: a. 名义上的, 名字的, 有名无实的, 稍许的\nn. 名词性词
- **英文释义**: existing in name only; very small or minimal; relating to a noun or noun phrase
- **词根词缀**: nomin(名字)+al(形容词)→名义上的
- **例句**:
- The company reported a nominal profit, but in reality it incurred substantial losses.
- She holds the nominal title of director, though she has no actual decision-making authority.
- **考频**: 8
## nominate

- **音标**: 'nɒmineit
- **释义**: vt. 提名, 任命, 命名\n[法] 提名...为候选人, 指定, 推荐
- **英文释义**: to formally propose someone for a position, award, or office
- **词根词缀**: nomin(名字)+ate(动词)→提名
- **例句**:
- The committee nominated her for the Nobel Prize in Literature.
- He was nominated as the party's presidential candidate after winning the primary election.
- **考频**: 9
## nickname

- **音标**: 'nikneim
- **释义**: n. 绰号, 昵称\nvt. 给...取绰号, 叫错名字\n[计] 绰号
- **英文释义**: a familiar or humorous name given to a person or thing instead of or as well as its real name
- **词根词缀**: nick(刻痕)+name(名字)→刻痕名字→绰号
- **例句**:
- Her nickname 'Sparky' reflects her energetic personality.
- He was nicknamed 'The Professor' by his teammates for his strategic thinking during the debate tournament.
- **考频**: 7
## noun

- **音标**: naun
- **释义**: n. 名词
- **英文释义**: A word that names a person, place, thing, idea, or quality.
- **词根词缀**: noun(名词)→名词
- **例句**:
- In the sentence 'The cat sat on the mat,' 'cat' and 'mat' are both nouns.
- Abstract nouns like 'justice' and 'freedom' are frequently discussed in academic essays on political philosophy.
- **考频**: 4
