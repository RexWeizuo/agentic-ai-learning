# 词根 magn/max (大与放大)

> **类型**: 词根

> **学习提示**: 表示巨大、放大或最大值的词根与近义词

**5 词**

---

## magnificent

- **音标**: mæg'nifisnt
- **释义**: a. 华丽的, 高尚的, 宏伟的
- **英文释义**: very impressive, beautiful, or grand in appearance, quality, or action
- **词根词缀**: magn(大)+ific(使)+ent(形容词)→宏大的
- **例句**:
- The palace boasts magnificent architecture and intricate frescoes.
- Her magnificent performance at the conference earned widespread acclaim from senior scholars.
- **考频**: 19
## magnify

- **音标**: 'mægnifai
- **释义**: vt. 放大, 扩大, 赞美, 夸大\nvi. 有放大能力
- **英文释义**: to make something appear larger, more important, or more intense than it really is
- **词根词缀**: magn(大)+ify(使)→放大
- **例句**:
- The microscope magnifies the specimen 1000 times, enabling detailed cellular observation.
- Media coverage tends to magnify minor policy disagreements into major political crises.
- **考频**: 16
## magnitude

- **音标**: 'mægnitju:d
- **释义**: n. 巨大, 重大, 重要, 大小, 数量, 星等(指星的亮度)\n[计] 大小; 值
- **英文释义**: The size, scale, or importance of something; in astronomy, a measure of the brightness of a celestial object.
- **词根词缀**: magn(大)+itude(名词)→大小
- **例句**:
- The magnitude of the economic crisis forced governments to implement unprecedented stimulus measures.
- Earthquakes are measured on the Richter scale, which quantifies their magnitude based on seismic energy released.
- **考频**: 5
## massive

- **音标**: 'mæsiv
- **释义**: a. 大而重的, 宽大的, 宏伟的\n[医] 大块的, 整块的, 大量的
- **英文释义**: very large in size, amount, or degree
- **词根词缀**: mass(大量)+ive(形容词)→大量的
- **例句**:
- The project required a massive investment of time and resources.
- She faced a massive challenge when she took over the failing department.
- **考频**: 75
## maximum

- **音标**: 'mæksimәn
- **释义**: n. 极点, 最大量, 极大\na. 最高的, 最大的, 最大极限的\n[计] 最大值
- **英文释义**: the greatest possible amount, degree, or value
- **词根词缀**: maxim(最大)+um(名词)→最大值
- **例句**:
- The engine operates at maximum efficiency when running at 3000 rpm.
- To minimize risk, the study set a maximum exposure time of two hours per day.
- **考频**: 58
