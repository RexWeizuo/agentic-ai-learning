# 词根：not- (标记，知道)

> **类型**: 词根

> **学习提示**: 共享词根 not-，表示“标记、知道、注意”。

**6 词**

---

## notable

- **音标**: 'nәutәbl
- **释义**: n. 著名人士, 值得注意之事物\na. 值得注意的, 显著的
- **英文释义**: worthy of attention; remarkable or distinguished
- **词根词缀**: not(标记)+able(能)→值得注意的
- **例句**:
- Her contributions to renewable energy research are truly notable.
- The conference attracted several political notables from across Europe.
- **考频**: 9
## note

- **音标**: nәut
- **释义**: n. 笔记, 记录, 注解, 票据, 符号, 显要, 注重, 便笺, 照会\nvt. 记录, 注解, 注意
- **英文释义**: A brief written record or comment; to observe or record something carefully.
- **词根词缀**: not(标记)+e→笔记
- **例句**:
- She made a note of the professor's key points during the lecture.
- The contract includes a special note stating that all payments must be made in advance.
- **考频**: 393
## notice

- **音标**: 'nәutis
- **释义**: n. 注意, 布告, 通知, 预告, 短评\nvt. 注意, 通知, 评论, 提及, 关注\nvi. 注意
- **英文释义**: n. the act of becoming aware of something; a written announcement or warning; vt. to become aware of, to point out, or to officially inform
- **词根词缀**: not(标记)+ice(名词)→通知
- **例句**:
- The sudden change in his tone did not escape my notice.
- Employees must be given at least 30 days' written notice before termination of their contracts.
- **考频**: 94
## notify

- **音标**: 'nәutifai
- **释义**: vt. 通知, 通告, 报告\n[计] 通知
- **英文释义**: to tell someone about something, especially officially or formally
- **词根词缀**: not(标记)+ify(使)→通知
- **例句**:
- The airline will notify passengers of any flight delays via SMS.
- Researchers must notify the ethics committee before initiating any human-subject experiments.
- **考频**: 8
## notion

- **音标**: 'nәuʃәn
- **释义**: n. 概念, 观念, 想法, 打算, 别致的小东西\n[经] 概念, 打算, 想法
- **英文释义**: an idea, belief, or opinion about something, often one that is not fully formed or is based on limited understanding
- **词根词缀**: not(标记)+ion(名词)→概念
- **例句**:
- She has a vague notion of how the stock market works, but lacks detailed knowledge.
- The notion that artificial intelligence will replace all human jobs is widely debated among economists.
- **考频**: 90
## notorious

- **音标**: nәu'tɒ:riәs
- **释义**: a. 声名狼藉的, 臭名昭著的, 众人皆知的
- **英文释义**: famous or well known, typically for some bad quality or deed
- **词根词缀**: not(标记)+orious(形容词)→臭名昭著的
- **例句**:
- The city is notorious for its high crime rate and traffic congestion.
- He became notorious after being implicated in a major financial scandal.
