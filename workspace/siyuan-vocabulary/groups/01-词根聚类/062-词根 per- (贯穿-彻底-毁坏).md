# 词根 per- (贯穿/彻底/毁坏)

> **类型**: 词根

> **学习提示**: per- 作前缀可表示“贯穿、彻底”或“毁坏”。permeate(渗透), perplex(彻底困惑), perish(彻底毁灭/死亡)。

**5 词**

---

## permeate

- **音标**: 'pә:mieit
- **释义**: vt. 弥漫, 渗透, 充满, 影响, 感染\nvi. 透入, 散布
- **英文释义**: to spread or flow throughout something, especially gradually and thoroughly
- **词根词缀**: per(贯穿)+mea(流动)+ate(动词)→流过→渗透
- **例句**:
- A sense of anxiety permeated the entire campus before the national entrance examination.
- New technologies are rapidly permeating traditional industries, transforming business models.
- **考频**: 10
## perplex

- **音标**: pә'pleks
- **释义**: vt. 使困惑, 使复杂化, 使为难\n[法] 困惑, 使复杂化, 使纠缠不清
- **英文释义**: to confuse or puzzle someone, especially by making something seem complicated or inexplicable
- **词根词缀**: per(完全)+plex(编织)→完全编织在一起→困惑
- **例句**:
- The sudden change in policy perplexed even the most experienced analysts.
- Her ambiguous response perplexed the committee, leaving them uncertain how to proceed.
- **考频**: 8
## persecute

- **音标**: 'pә:sikju:t
- **释义**: vt. 迫害, 虐待, 困扰, 同...捣乱\n[法] 迫害, 虐待, 烦扰
- **英文释义**: to treat someone unfairly or cruelly, especially because of their race, religion, political beliefs, or other characteristics
- **词根词缀**: per(贯穿/完全/每)+secut(follow)+e
- **例句**:
- Minority groups have historically been persecuted for their religious beliefs.
- The journalist was persecuted by the authoritarian regime for exposing government corruption.
## perish

- **音标**: 'periʃ
- **释义**: vi. 毁灭, 丧生, 凋谢, 颓丧, 死亡, 腐烂\nvt. 毁坏, 使麻木, 使丧生, 耗尽
- **英文释义**: to die, be destroyed, or decay, especially suddenly or violently
- **词根词缀**: per(完全)+ish(去)→完全离去→消亡
- **例句**:
- Many ancient manuscripts perished in the fire that consumed the library.
- Without refrigeration, perishable foods such as meat and dairy will quickly perish.
- **考频**: 9
## perceive

- **音标**: pә'si:v
- **释义**: vt. 感觉, 认知, 理解, 意识到
- **英文释义**: to become aware of something through the senses or the mind; to understand or interpret something in a particular way
- **词根词缀**: per(通过)+ceive(拿)→通过感官拿→感知
- **例句**:
- She perceived a subtle shift in his tone, suggesting he was no longer fully committed to the plan.
- Many economists perceive the recent policy change as a necessary step toward long-term fiscal stability.
- **考频**: 77
