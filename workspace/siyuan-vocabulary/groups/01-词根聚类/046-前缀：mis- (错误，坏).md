# 前缀：mis- (错误，坏)

> **类型**: 词根

> **学习提示**: 共享前缀 mis-，表示“错误、坏、否定”。

**5 词**

---

## mischief

- **音标**: 'mistʃif
- **释义**: n. 损害, 伤害, 灾祸, 恶作剧, 淘气\n[法] 损害, 伤害, 危害
- **英文释义**: playful or slightly malicious behavior that causes minor trouble or annoyance
- **词根词缀**: mis(错误/坏)+chief
- **例句**:
- The children's mischief left the classroom in complete chaos before the teacher arrived.
- Though intended as a harmless prank, his mischief inadvertently damaged the lab equipment, drawing serious disciplinary attention.
## misfortune

- **音标**: mis'fɒ:tʃәn
- **释义**: n. 不幸, 灾祸, 坏运气\n[法] 不幸事故, 不幸, 灾祸
- **英文释义**: bad luck or an unfortunate event that causes suffering or loss
- **词根词缀**: mis(坏)+fortune(运气)→不幸
- **例句**:
- His business failure was a great misfortune, but he remained resilient and started anew.
- The earthquake was a profound misfortune for the region, destroying homes and displacing thousands.
- **考频**: 28
## mislead

- **音标**: mis'li:d
- **释义**: vt. 误导
- **英文释义**: to cause someone to have a wrong idea or belief
- **词根词缀**: mis(错误)+lead(引导)→误导
- **例句**:
- The misleading advertisement misled consumers into thinking the product was environmentally friendly.
- Historical documents can mislead researchers if taken out of context.
- **考频**: 65
## mistake

- **音标**: mis'teik
- **释义**: n. 错误, 误会\nvi. 犯错, 误认\nvt. 误解, 弄错\n[计] 错误
- **英文释义**: an error in action, judgment, or understanding
- **词根词缀**: mis(错误)+take(拿)→错误
- **例句**:
- She realized her mistake only after submitting the report.
- He mistook the quiet student for being indifferent, when in fact she was deeply engaged.
- **考频**: 198
## misunderstand

- **音标**: .misʌndә'stænd
- **释义**: vt. 误解, 误会
- **英文释义**: to fail to understand something correctly
- **词根词缀**: mis(错误)+understand(理解)→误解
- **例句**:
- I think there's been a misunderstanding—we never agreed to the revised deadline.
- She misunderstood the professor's instructions and submitted the wrong format for her thesis proposal.
- **考频**: 5
