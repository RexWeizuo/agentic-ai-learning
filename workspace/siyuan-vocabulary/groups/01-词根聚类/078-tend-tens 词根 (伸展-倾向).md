# tend/tens 词根 (伸展/倾向)

> **类型**: 词根

> **学习提示**: 词根 tend/tens 表示“伸展、倾向”。tension (紧张/张力), tentative (试验性的/犹豫的)。

**4 词**

---

## tend

- **音标**: tend
- **释义**: vi. 走向, 有某种的倾向, 易于, 照顾, 注意\nvt. 照料, 护理
- **英文释义**: to have a general tendency or inclination to behave or develop in a particular way; to care for or look after someone or something
- **词根词缀**: tend(伸展)→趋向
- **例句**:
- Young adults tend to prioritize career advancement over early marriage.
- Nurses are trained to tend patients with both medical expertise and compassion.
- **考频**: 469
## tendency

- **音标**: 'tendәnsi
- **释义**: n. 趋向, 倾向\n[医] 趋向, 趋势
- **英文释义**: a natural inclination or likelihood to behave or develop in a particular way
- **词根词缀**: tend(伸展)+ency(名词)→趋势
- **例句**:
- There is a growing tendency among young professionals to prioritize work-life balance over rapid career advancement.
- Studies have shown a strong tendency for patients with hypertension to develop cardiovascular complications if left untreated.
- **考频**: 57
## tension

- **音标**: 'tenʃәn
- **释义**: n. 紧张, 不安, 拉紧, 张力, 压力, 电压\nvt. 拉紧, 使紧张
- **英文释义**: The state of being stretched tight; mental or emotional strain; electrical potential difference measured in volts.
- **词根词缀**: tens(伸展)+ion(名词)→紧张
- **例句**:
- The tension between the two rival companies has escalated into a public legal battle.
- High voltage tension in the transmission lines requires strict safety protocols.
- **考频**: 41
## tentative

- **音标**: 'tentәtiv
- **释义**: n. 试验\na. 试验性的, 暂时的
- **英文释义**: not certain or final; provisional
- **词根词缀**: tent(尝试)+ative(形容词)→尝试的
- **例句**:
- The committee made a tentative agreement pending further review.
- She offered a tentative explanation for the unexpected results, acknowledging that more data was needed.
- **考频**: 9
