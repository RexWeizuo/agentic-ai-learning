# exceed/excess 超越/过度

> **类型**: 词根

> **学习提示**: 词根 ced/cess/cel 表示超越、过度、优秀

**5 词**

---

## exceed

- **音标**: ik'si:d
- **释义**: vt. 超过, 超越, 胜过\nvi. 超过其他
- **英文释义**: to go beyond a limit, amount, or degree
- **词根词缀**: ex(出)+ceed(走)→走出→超过
- **例句**:
- Her test score exceeded the class average by 15 points.
- The company's profits this quarter exceeded expectations despite the economic downturn.
- **考频**: 46
## exceedingly

- **音标**: ik'si:diŋli
- **释义**: adv. 极端地, 极度地, 非常
- **英文释义**: to an extreme degree; extremely
- **词根词缀**: ex(出)+ceed(走)+ing(形容词)+ly(副词)→超过地→非常
- **例句**:
- The research findings were exceedingly complex and required expert interpretation.
- Her dedication to the project was exceedingly evident in the meticulous attention to detail.
- **考频**: 3
## excess

- **音标**: ik'ses
- **释义**: n. 过度, 剩于, 超过, 超额\na. 过量的, 额外的
- **英文释义**: an amount or degree that is more than necessary, permitted, or desirable
- **词根词缀**: ex(出)+cess(走)→走出界限→过量
- **例句**:
- The company reported a significant excess of inventory over demand.
- Chronic stress and excess screen time are linked to declining mental health in adolescents.
- **考频**: 39
## excessive

- **音标**: ik'sesiv
- **释义**: a. 过度的, 过多的, 极端的\n[法] 过度的, 过分的, 额外的
- **英文释义**: beyond what is normal, reasonable, or necessary
- **词根词缀**: ex(出)+cess(走)+ive(形容词)→走出界限的→过度的
- **例句**:
- The patient suffered from excessive bleeding after the surgery.
- Excessive use of social media has been linked to increased anxiety among college students.
- **考频**: 86
## excel

- **音标**: ik'sel
- **释义**: vt. 胜过, 优于, 擅长\nvi. 胜过其他
- **英文释义**: to be exceptionally good at something; to perform better than others
- **词根词缀**: ex(出)+cel(升起)→升出→胜过
- **例句**:
- Her analytical skills enable her to excel in data-driven research.
- Students who excel academically often combine disciplined study habits with critical thinking.
- **考频**: 23
