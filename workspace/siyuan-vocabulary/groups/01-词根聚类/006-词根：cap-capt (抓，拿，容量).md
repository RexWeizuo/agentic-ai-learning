# 词根：cap/capt (抓，拿，容量)

> **类型**: 词根

> **学习提示**: cap/capt/cept 词根表示“拿、抓、容纳”，注意 capacity 兼有“容量”和“能力”之意。

**4 词**

---

## capable

- **音标**: /ˈkeɪpəbl/
- **词性**: adj.
- **释义**: 有能力的，能干的
- **英文释义**: having the ability, fitness, or quality necessary to do or achieve something
- **同义词**: competent, able, efficient, skilled
- **反义词**: incapable, incompetent, unable
- **词根词缀**: cap(拿)+able→能拿住的→有能力的
- **搭配**: capable of, highly capable, perfectly capable
- **例句**:
- She is a capable manager.
- He is capable of solving this problem.
- **考频**: 96
## capacity

- **音标**: kә'pæsiti
- **释义**: n. 容量, 能力, 才能, 资格\n[计] 容量
- **英文释义**: the maximum amount that something can contain or produce; or the ability to do something
- **词根词缀**: cap(拿)+acity(名词)→能拿住→容量
- **例句**:
- The stadium has a seating capacity of 50,000.
- She demonstrated exceptional leadership capacity during the crisis, making her a strong candidate for promotion.
- **考频**: 147
## captive

- **音标**: 'kæptiv
- **释义**: n. 俘虏, 迷恋者\na. 被俘的, 被迷住的
- **英文释义**: a person who is confined or held against their will; or someone deeply fascinated or obsessed by something
- **词根词缀**: capt(抓)+ive(形容词)→被抓的→俘虏
- **例句**:
- The negotiators demanded the immediate release of all captives held by the rebel group.
- She became a captive audience to his endless monologue on quantum physics.
- **考频**: 10
## capture

- **音标**: 'kæptʃә
- **释义**: n. 抓取, 战利品, 捕获之物\nvt. 抓取, 获得, 迷住\n[计] 截获命令
- **英文释义**: to take or secure something, especially by force or skill; to attract and hold someone's attention or interest
- **词根词缀**: capt(抓)+ure(名词/动词)→抓→捕获
- **例句**:
- The documentary successfully captures the complexity of urban migration.
- Her innovative approach to renewable energy captured the judges' attention at the international science fair.
- **考频**: 72
