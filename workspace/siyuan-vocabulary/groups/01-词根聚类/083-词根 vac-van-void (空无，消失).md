# 词根 vac/van/void (空无，消失)

> **类型**: 词根

> **学习提示**: 表示“空、虚无”，vanity 引申为“虚荣（空虚的骄傲）”，void 常作法律术语表示“无效的”。

**5 词**

---

## vacant

- **音标**: 'veikәnt
- **释义**: a. 空的, 空白的, 空虚的, 空闲的, 茫然的, 空缺的\n[法] 空的, 空虚的, 闲暇的
- **英文释义**: empty, not occupied or filled; unoccupied (e.g., a position or room); lacking thought or expression
- **词根词缀**: vac(空)+ant(形容词)→空的
- **例句**:
- The office manager checked the schedule to see if any meeting rooms were vacant.
- Her vacant stare suggested she hadn’t absorbed a word of the professor’s complex argument.
- **考频**: 16
## vacuum

- **音标**: ''vækjuәm
- **释义**: n. 真空, 空间, 真空吸尘器\na. 真空的, 产生真空的, 利用真空的\nvt. 用吸尘器打扫
- **英文释义**: A space entirely empty of matter; also, a device that cleans surfaces using suction.
- **词根词缀**: vac(空)+uum(名词)→真空
- **例句**:
- The experiment must be conducted in a high vacuum to prevent interference from air molecules.
- She used the vacuum to clean the carpet before the guests arrived.
- **考频**: 7
## vanish

- **音标**: 'væniʃ
- **释义**: vi. 消失, 不见, 成为零
- **英文释义**: to disappear suddenly and completely, often in a way that is mysterious or unexplained
- **词根词缀**: van(空)+ish(动词后缀)→变空→消失
- **例句**:
- The suspect vanished without a trace after the robbery.
- All evidence of the ancient civilization has virtually vanished over centuries of erosion and war.
- **考频**: 19
## vanity

- **音标**: 'væniti
- **释义**: n. 虚荣心, 虚无, 自负, 空虚, 时髦的小摆饰
- **英文释义**: Excessive pride in one's appearance, abilities, or achievements; also, the quality of being empty or without real value.
- **词根词缀**: van(空)+ity(名词后缀)→空虚→虚荣
- **例句**:
- Her constant need for praise revealed a deep-seated vanity that undermined her professional credibility.
- The philosopher argued that all earthly glory is mere vanity — transient and ultimately meaningless.
- **考频**: 3
## void

- **音标**: vɒid
- **释义**: n. 空虚, 空间, 空白, 空隙, 空虚感\na. 空的, 无效的, 无人的, 没有的, 无用的\n[计] 脱墨; 空白点
- **英文释义**: n. an empty space or feeling of emptiness; a legal or formal invalidity; a. having no legal force, effect, or validity
- **词根词缀**: void(空)→空的→无效的
- **例句**:
- The contract was declared void after it was discovered that one party had committed fraud.
- She felt a profound void after losing her closest friend, as if a part of her identity had vanished.
- **考频**: 10
