# 主题：规则、义务与道德

> **类型**: 主题

> **学习提示**: 表示道德规范、法律义务、誓言及价值评判的词汇。

**7 词**

---

## obligation

- **音标**: .ɒbli'geiʃәn
- **释义**: n. 义务, 责任, 约束, 契约, 恩惠, 债务\n[经] 待付款, 债务, 义务
- **英文释义**: A moral or legal duty to do something, or a binding agreement that requires payment or performance.
- **词根词缀**: ob(加强)+lig(绑)+ation(名词)→义务
- **例句**:
- Fulfilling contractual obligations is essential for maintaining business credibility.
- As a citizen, she felt a strong moral obligation to vote in the national election.
- **考频**: 41
## oblige

- **音标**: ә'blaidʒ
- **释义**: vt. 强制, 施恩惠于, 使感激\nvi. 施恩惠, 帮忙
- **英文释义**: to compel by law, duty, or circumstances; to do a favor for someone
- **词根词缀**: ob(加强)+lig(捆绑)+e→捆绑在一起→迫使
- **例句**:
- The contract obliges both parties to maintain confidentiality.
- She felt obliged to help her colleague finish the report before the deadline.
- **考频**: 41
## oath

- **音标**: әuθ
- **释义**: n. 誓约, 誓言, 咒骂\n[法] 誓言, 誓约, 宣誓
- **英文释义**: a formal promise, often made publicly or under oath in a legal context, to do or not do something
- **词根词缀**: oath(誓言)→誓言
- **例句**:
- The witness took an oath to tell the truth before testifying in court.
- She swore an oath of allegiance to the constitution upon becoming a naturalized citizen.
- **考频**: 1
## norm

- **音标**: nɒ:m
- **释义**: n. 基准, 模范, 标准, 准则, 平均数\n[化] 定额
- **英文释义**: A standard, pattern, or average level of behavior, performance, or measurement that is typical or expected in a particular context.
- **词根词缀**: norm(标准)→标准
- **例句**:
- It is now the norm for employees to work remotely at least two days a week.
- The study found that the average reaction time was significantly below the established clinical norm.
- **考频**: 45
## moral

- **音标**: 'mɒrәl
- **释义**: n. 道德, 品行, 寓意\na. 道德的, 品性端正的, 精神上的
- **英文释义**: relating to principles of right and wrong behavior, or to the ability to distinguish between right and wrong
- **词根词缀**: mor(风俗)+al(形容词)→风俗的→道德的
- **例句**:
- The novel conveys a powerful moral about the consequences of unchecked ambition.
- She demonstrated remarkable moral courage by speaking out against corruption despite personal risk.
- **考频**: 142
## morality

- **音标**: mә'ræliti
- **释义**: n. 道德, 教训, 品行\n[法] 道德, 道义
- **英文释义**: the principles concerning the distinction between right and wrong or good and bad behavior
- **词根词缀**: moral(道德)+ity(名词)→道德
- **例句**:
- The novel explores themes of justice, power, and morality in a corrupt society.
- Her decision to resign was guided by a strong sense of personal morality, despite potential career consequences.
- **考频**: 18
## merit

- **音标**: 'merit
- **释义**: n. 优点, 功绩, 价值, 功过, 真相\nvt. 值得\nvi. 应受
- **英文释义**: the quality of being good, valuable, or deserving praise or reward
- **词根词缀**: merit(值得)→优点
- **例句**:
- Her proposal was accepted on its merits, not because of her seniority.
- The committee concluded that the candidate's research did not merit publication in a top-tier journal.
- **考频**: 73
