## abdomen

- **音标**: 'æbdәmen
- **词性**: n.
- **释义**: ==n. 腹部\n[医] 腹[部]==
- **英文释义**: n. the region of the body of a vertebrate between the thorax and the pelvis
- **同义词**: belly, tummy, abdominal region
- **词根词缀**: ab-(away from)+domen(from Latin 'domus' meaning 'house')→literally 'away from the house (i.e., the thoracic 'home' of vital organs)'→refers to the cavity below the diaphragm, housing digestive organs
- **搭配**: abdominal muscles, abdomen pain, upper abdomen
- **例句**:
- The surgeon made an incision in the abdomen to access the appendix.
- She felt a sharp pain in her lower abdomen after the workout.
- **考频**: 1
## artery

- **音标**: 'ɑ:tәri
- **释义**: ==n. 动脉, 干道, 要道\n[医] 动脉==
- **英文释义**: A blood vessel that carries oxygenated blood from the heart to the body tissues (except pulmonary artery); also used metaphorically for a major route or channel.
- **词根词缀**: art(动脉)+ery→动脉
- **例句**:
- The coronary artery supplies blood to the heart muscle, and its blockage can lead to a myocardial infarction.
- During rush hour, Nanjing Road functions as a vital artery of Shanghai's urban transportation system.
- **考频**: 2
## ankle

- **音标**: 'æŋkl
- **释义**: ==n. 踝\n[医] 踝, 踝关节==
- **英文释义**: The joint connecting the foot to the leg, where the tibia and fibula meet the talus bone.
- **词根词缀**: ank(bend)+le(small)→small bend→脚踝
- **例句**:
- She sprained her ankle while running on uneven terrain, causing swelling and limited mobility.
- During the physical examination, the doctor assessed the range of motion in both ankles to rule out ligament damage.
- **考频**: 4
## appetite

- **音标**: 'æpitait
- **释义**: ==n. 食欲, 欲望, 爱好\n[医] 食欲==
- **英文释义**: A natural desire to satisfy a bodily need, especially for food; also used figuratively for a strong liking or craving for something.
- **同义词**: hunger, craving
- **词根词缀**: ap(朝向)+pet(追求)+ite→追求食物→食欲
- **例句**:
- Her rigorous training regimen has significantly increased her appetite for both physical challenge and academic rigor.
- The economic recovery has rekindled investors' appetite for riskier assets, such as emerging-market equities.
- **考频**: 35
## adolescent

- **音标**: .ædәu'lesәnt
- **词性**: ['n.', 'adj.']
- **释义**: ==a. 青春期的, 青少年的\nn. 青少年==
- **英文释义**: n. a juvenile between the onset of puberty and maturity\na. relating to or peculiar to or suggestive of an adolescent\ns. being of the age 13 through 19\ns. displaying or suggesting a lack of maturity
- **同义词**: teenager, youth, young person
- **反义词**: adult, senior
- **词根词缀**: ad-(to)+olesc-(grow, from Latin 'olescere', inchoative of 'alere' to nourish/grow)+-ent(acting, present participle suffix)→ 'growing toward maturity' → a person in the transitional stage from childhood to adulthood, or pertaining to that stage
- **搭配**: adolescent development, adolescent psychology, adolescent health
- **例句**:
- The adolescent showed remarkable independence in managing his studies.
- Adolescent behavior often reflects ongoing brain development and social learning.
- **考频**: 68
## adult

- **音标**: 'ædʌlt
- **词性**: n./adj.
- **释义**: ==n. 成人, 成虫\na. 成年的, 成熟的==
- **英文释义**: n. a fully developed person from maturity onward\nn. any mature animal\ns. (of animals) fully developed
- **同义词**: grown-up, mature person, legal adult
- **反义词**: child, minor
- **词根词缀**: ad-(to, toward)+ult(coming last, final)→'coming to the final stage of development'→a fully developed human or organism; hence 'adult'
- **搭配**: adult education, adult content, adult responsibilities
- **例句**:
- All adults must show valid identification to enter the venue.
- The butterfly reaches its adult stage after emerging from the chrysalis.
- **考频**: 353
## animal

- **音标**: 'ænimәl
- **释义**: ==n. 动物\n[医] 动物==
- **英文释义**: a living organism that can move voluntarily, typically feeds on organic matter, and lacks cell walls and chlorophyll
- **词根词缀**: anima(生命)+l→有生命的→动物
- **例句**:
- The study compared the neural responses of humans and other animals to visual stimuli.
- As a vertebrate animal, the dolphin exhibits complex social behavior and advanced cognitive abilities.
- **考频**: 511
