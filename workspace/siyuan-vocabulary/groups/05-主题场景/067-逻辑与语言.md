## logic

- **音标**: 'lɒdʒik
- **释义**: n. 逻辑, 逻辑学, 推理的方法, 推理, 逻辑性\n[计] 逻辑
- **英文释义**: The science of reasoning correctly, or the principles governing valid inference and demonstration.
- **词根词缀**: log(语言/理性)+ic(名词)→逻辑
- **例句**:
- The argument lacks logical coherence—the conclusion does not follow from the premises by sound logic.
- In computer science, Boolean logic forms the foundation of digital circuit design and programming.
- **考频**: 14
## logical

- **音标**: 'lɒdʒikәl
- **释义**: a. 合乎逻辑的, 合理的\n[法] 逻辑的, 符合逻辑的
- **英文释义**: based on clear reasoning and sound evidence; consistent with the principles of logic
- **词根词缀**: log(语言/理性)+ical(形容词)→合乎逻辑的
- **例句**:
- Her argument was so well-structured and evidence-based that it struck everyone as perfectly logical.
- It is not logically necessary to assume causation from correlation—a point frequently tested in GRE and postgraduate English exams.
- **考频**: 36
## linguistic

- **音标**: liŋ'^wistik
- **释义**: a. 语言的, 语言学的\n[计] 语言的, 语言学的
- **英文释义**: relating to language or the scientific study of language
- **词根词缀**: lingu(语言)+ist(人)+ic(形容词)→语言学的
- **例句**:
- Her linguistic competence enabled her to interpret complex academic texts in three languages.
- The rise of bilingual education reflects growing recognition of linguistic diversity in modern classrooms.
- **考频**: 36
## language

- **音标**: 'læŋgwidʒ
- **释义**: n. 语言, 文字, 措辞\n[计] 语言
- **英文释义**: A system of communication consisting of words, either spoken or written, used by a particular community or country.
- **词根词缀**: langu(词根:舌头)+age(名词)→舌头→语言
- **例句**:
- Mandarin Chinese is the most widely spoken language in the world.
- The programming language Python is known for its simplicity and readability.
