## hormone

- **音标**: 'hɒ:mәun
- **释义**: n. 荷尔蒙\n[化] 激素(旧称荷尔蒙)
- **英文释义**: A chemical substance produced in an organism that regulates physiological activities and behavior by being transported in the blood to target organs or tissues.
- **词根词缀**: hormone(激素)→激素
- **例句**:
- Cortisol, a stress-related hormone, can impair memory formation when present in excessive amounts.
- The onset of puberty is triggered by a surge in gonadotropin-releasing hormone (GnRH) from the hypothalamus.
- **考频**: 26
## hygiene

- **音标**: 'haidʒi:n
- **释义**: n. 卫生保健, 卫生学, 保健法\n[医] 卫生, 卫生学
- **英文释义**: The science and practice of maintaining health through cleanliness and healthy habits.
- **词根词缀**: hygie(健康)+ne→健康学→卫生
- **例句**:
- Good personal hygiene, such as regular handwashing, is essential to prevent the spread of infectious diseases.
- Public health policies increasingly emphasize environmental hygiene and access to clean water in low-income communities.
- **考频**: 9
## immune

- **音标**: i'mju:n
- **释义**: a. 免疫的, 免除的, 不受影响的\nn. 免疫者
- **英文释义**: not affected by or protected against a disease, influence, or obligation
- **词根词缀**: im(不)+mun(服务)+e→不服务→免疫的
- **例句**:
- People with strong immune systems are often immune to common cold viruses.
- Senior executives were effectively immune to the new compliance regulations due to ambiguous wording.
- **考频**: 45
## infect

- **音标**: in'fekt
- **释义**: vt. 传染, 感染\n[医] 传染, 感染
- **英文释义**: to cause a disease to spread to someone or something
- **词根词缀**: in(向内)+fect(做)→做进去→感染
- **例句**:
- The virus can infect both humans and animals.
- Poor hygiene practices may infect wounds with bacteria, delaying recovery.
- **考频**: 20
## infectious

- **音标**: in'fekʃәs
- **释义**: a. 有传染性的, 易传染的\n[医] 传染性的
- **英文释义**: Capable of spreading from one person or organism to another, especially through pathogens; also used figuratively to describe something that spreads easily (e.g., emotions or enthusiasm).
- **词根词缀**: in(向内)+fect(做)+ious(形容词)→感染的→传染性的
- **例句**:
- The virus is highly infectious and can spread rapidly in crowded environments.
- Her enthusiasm for classical literature was infectious, inspiring many students to enroll in the course.
- **考频**: 11
## influenza

- **音标**: .influ'enzә
- **释义**: n. 流行性感冒\n[医] 流行性感冒, 流感
- **英文释义**: An acute viral infection of the respiratory tract, highly contagious and often epidemic.
- **词根词缀**: in(不/无/进入)+flu(flow)+enza→流
- **例句**:
- The university suspended classes after an influenza outbreak affected over 200 students.
- Annual vaccination is strongly recommended to reduce the risk of severe complications from influenza.

## inject

- **音标**: in'dʒekt
- **释义**: vt. 注射, 注入, 使入轨\n[医] 注射
- **英文释义**: to introduce a substance, idea, or quality into something, especially using a syringe or in a forceful or sudden way
- **词根词缀**: in(向内)+ject(投)→投入→注射
- **例句**:
