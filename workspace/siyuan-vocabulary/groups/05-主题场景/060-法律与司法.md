# 法律与司法

> **类型**: 主题

## judgment

- **音标**: 'dʒʌdʒmәnt
- **释义**: n. 裁判, 宣告, 判决书\n[医] 判断
- **英文释义**: The ability to make considered decisions or come to sensible conclusions; a formal decision given by a court of law.
- **词根词缀**: judg(判断)+ment(名词)→判断，判决
- **例句**:
- The judge's judgment in the landmark case set a new legal precedent.
- Her sound judgment and analytical rigor made her an outstanding candidate for the clinical research position.
- **考频**: 61
## judicial

- **音标**: dʒu:'diʃәl
- **释义**: a. 法庭的, 公正的, 审判上的, 司法的\n[法] 司法的, 审判上的, 法官的
- **英文释义**: relating to courts of law, judges, or the administration of justice
- **词根词缀**: judic(判断)+ial(形容词)→司法的
- **例句**:
- The Supreme Court's decision carries significant judicial weight in constitutional matters.
- A fair trial requires impartial judicial oversight and adherence to due process.
- **考频**: 2
## jurisdiction

- **音标**: .dʒuәris'dikʃәn
- **释义**: n. 司法权, 审判权, 管辖权\n[经] 法律管辖权, 审判权
- **英文释义**: The official power to make legal decisions and judgments; the authority of a court or government body to interpret and apply the law.
- **词根词缀**: juris(法律)+dict(说)+ion→法律所说→司法权
- **例句**:
- The U.S. Supreme Court has original jurisdiction in cases involving ambassadors and disputes between states.
- Because the contract was signed in Germany, German courts have exclusive jurisdiction over any related legal disputes.
- **考频**: 4
## jury

- **音标**: 'dʒuәri
- **释义**: n. 陪审团, 评判委员会\na. 应急的
- **英文释义**: A group of people sworn to render a verdict in a legal case or to judge a competition.
- **词根词缀**: jur(法律)+y→法律团体→陪审团
- **例句**:
- The jury deliberated for three days before reaching a unanimous verdict.
- She was selected to serve on the jury for a high-profile criminal trial.
- **考频**: 45
## lawsuit

- **音标**: 'lɒ:sju:t
- **释义**: n. 诉讼\n[法] 诉讼, 诉讼案件
- **英文释义**: A legal action brought in a court of law to resolve a dispute or enforce a right.
- **词根词缀**: law(词根:法律)+suit(词根:诉讼)→法律诉讼→诉讼
- **例句**:
- The company faced a multimillion-dollar lawsuit after allegedly violating environmental regulations.
- Filing a lawsuit is often a last resort when contractual disputes cannot be settled through negotiation.
- **考频**: 29
## magistrate

- **音标**: 'mædʒistreit
- **释义**: n. 长官, 法官, 推事\n[法] 司法行政官, 治安法官, 地方法官
- **英文释义**: A civil officer empowered to administer the law, especially a local judge with limited jurisdiction.
- **词根词缀**: ag(ag/act)+istr+-ate(使…/…的/…的人)→做
- **例句**:
- The magistrate dismissed the case due to insufficient evidence.
- In England, a magistrate often handles minor criminal offenses without a jury trial.
## liability

- **音标**: laiә'biliti
- **释义**: n. 责任, 债务, 倾向\n[经] 责任, 义务, 负债
- **英文释义**: Something for which a person or organization is legally responsible, especially a debt or obligation; also, a disadvantage or weakness.
- **词根词缀**: li(绑)+abil(能)+ity(名词)→能被绑住的状态→责任
- **例句**:
- The company's current liabilities include short-term loans and unpaid invoices.
- His lack of experience was seen as a liability in the competitive job market.
- **考频**: 29
## liable

- **音标**: 'laiәbl
- **释义**: a. 有义务的, 应负责的, 有...倾向的\n[法] 有责任的, 可受处理的, 应受罚的
- **英文释义**: legally responsible for something, especially for paying money or being punished; also used to indicate a tendency to do something
- **词根词缀**: li(绑)+able(能)→能被绑住的→有责任的
- **例句**:
- Companies found guilty of environmental violations are liable to heavy fines.
- He is liable to forget important deadlines when under stress.
- **考频**: 26
