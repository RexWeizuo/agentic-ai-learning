# 核心、关键、标准

> **类型**: 主题

> **学习提示**: 表示事物的核心要素、决定性因素或恒定标准。

**4 词**

---

## CORE

- **音标**: kɒ:
- **释义**: n. 核心, 果心, 要点\nvt. 挖...的核\n[计] 内核, 核心网
- **英文释义**: The central, most important, or essential part of something; also, to remove the core from (e.g., fruit).
- **词根词缀**: core(核心)→核心
- **例句**:
- The core curriculum includes courses in mathematics, science, and literature.
- Before baking the apple pie, she used a corer to core the apples efficiently.
- **考频**: 53
## crucial

- **音标**: 'kru:ʃәl
- **释义**: a. 决定性的, 重要的, 严厉的\n[医] 十字形的; 决断的, 定局的
- **英文释义**: extremely important or necessary, especially because it will affect the success or failure of something
- **词根词缀**: cruc(十字)+ial(形容词)→十字路口的关键→关键的
- **例句**:
- The timing of the policy announcement was crucial to its public reception.
- In clinical trials, patient adherence to the protocol is crucial for obtaining valid results.
- **考频**: 59
## criterion

- **音标**: krai'tiәriәn
- **释义**: n. 标准, 准则, 规范\n[化] 判据
- **英文释义**: A principle or standard by which something may be judged or decided
- **词根词缀**: criter(判断)+ion(名词)→判断标准→标准
- **例句**:
- The primary criterion for admission to the program is academic excellence.
- Environmental impact has become a key criterion in evaluating large-scale infrastructure projects.
- **考频**: 29
## constant

- **音标**: 'kɒnstәnt
- **释义**: n. 常数, 恒量\na. 不变的, 一定的, 时常的\n[计] 常量; 常数; 恒值
- **英文释义**: A value that does not change; unvarying or steadfast in nature, purpose, or degree.
- **词根词缀**: con(加强)+stant(站立)→始终站立→不变的
- **例句**:
- The speed of light in a vacuum is a fundamental physical constant.
- Despite the company's financial volatility, her commitment to ethical practice remained constant.
- **考频**: 75
