# 商业、交易与财务

> **类型**: 主题

> **学习提示**: 经济类阅读核心词，注意 quote(报价/引用) 与 quota(配额) 的形近义异。

**8 词**

---

## purchase

- **音标**: 'pә:tʃәs
- **释义**: n. 购买, 购买品, 紧握, 绞辘\nvt. 购买, 赢得, 努力取得, 用滑轮起(锚等)
- **英文释义**: The act of buying something; a thing that has been bought; or, in mechanics, a mechanical advantage gained by a system of pulleys.
- **词根词缀**: pur(向前)+chase(追逐)→向前追逐→购买
- **例句**:
- The company announced a major purchase of renewable energy assets to meet its sustainability goals.
- She managed to secure the contract through careful negotiation and strategic purchase of key stakeholder support.
- **考频**: 178
## quota

- **音标**: 'kwәutә
- **释义**: n. 配额, 限额\n[化] 定额
- **英文释义**: A fixed share or limit of something, often set by authority for distribution or production.
- **词根词缀**: quot(多少)+a(名词)→配额
- **例句**:
- The government imposed an import quota on steel to protect domestic industries.
- Each department has a strict hiring quota that must not be exceeded this fiscal year.
- **考频**: 26
## quote

- **音标**: kwәut
- **释义**: n. 引用\nvt. 引述, 举证, 报(价)\nvi. 引用
- **英文释义**: to repeat or copy words from a source, or to state a price for a service or product
- **词根词缀**: quot(多少)+e→引用
- **例句**:
- The essay opens with a powerful quote from Shakespeare to underscore the theme of moral ambiguity.
- Before signing the contract, the contractor was required to quote a fixed price for the renovation work.
- **考频**: 54
## retail

- **音标**: 'ri:teil
- **释义**: n. 零售\na. 零售的\nvt. 零售, 详述, 传播\nvi. 零售
- **英文释义**: the sale of goods or services directly to consumers for their personal use
- **词根词缀**: re(再次)+tail(切割)→切碎→零售
- **例句**:
- The company reported a 5% increase in retail sales during the holiday season.
- She decided to retail the product through online platforms rather than wholesale channels.
- **考频**: 72
## revenue

- **音标**: 'revinju:
- **释义**: n. 收入, 岁入, 税收, 税务局\n[经] 岁入, 税收, 税务局
- **英文释义**: The income that a company, organization, or government receives from taxes, sales, or other sources.
- **词根词缀**: re(回)+ven(来)+ue(名词)→回来的东西→收入
- **例句**:
- The tech startup's revenue doubled after launching its new subscription service.
- Government revenue from corporate taxes declined sharply during the economic downturn.
- **考频**: 113
## rent

- **音标**: rent
- **释义**: n. 租金, 房租, 出租物, 裂缝, 破裂处, 分裂\nvt. 租用, 租出\nvi. 出租\na. 分裂的, 破裂的\nrend 的过去式和过去分词
- **英文释义**: money paid regularly for the use of property, especially a house or apartment; also, to hire or lease something
- **词根词缀**: re(再次)+nt(给)→再次给→租金
- **例句**:
- The tenant failed to pay the monthly rent on time, risking eviction.
- Many graduate students in Beijing struggle to afford rent in university-adjacent neighborhoods.
- **考频**: 109
## repay

- **音标**: ri'pei
- **释义**: v. 偿还, 报答, 报复
- **英文释义**: to pay back money that has been borrowed; to return a favor or harm
- **词根词缀**: re(再次)+pay(支付)→再次支付→偿还
- **例句**:
- She worked extra hours to repay the loan she had taken from her parents.
- His kindness was repaid with unwavering loyalty, though some acts of betrayal were later repaid in kind.
- **考频**: 8
## refund

- **音标**: 'ri:fʌnd
- **释义**: n. 偿还\nvt. 付还, 偿还借款\nvi. 归还, 偿还
- **英文释义**: The act of returning money to someone, typically because a product or service was unsatisfactory, cancelled, or overpaid.
- **词根词缀**: re(回)+fund(资金)→资金回→退款
- **例句**:
- The airline issued a full refund after the flight was cancelled due to severe weather.
- Students who withdraw from the course before the deadline are eligible for a partial refund.
- **考频**: 7
