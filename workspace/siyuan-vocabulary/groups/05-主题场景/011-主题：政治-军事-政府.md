# 主题：政治/军事/政府

> **类型**: 主题

> **学习提示**: 政治场景词汇：candidate(候选人), chancellor(总理/大臣), coalition(联合政府), charter(宪章/特许状)。

**6 词**

---

## campaign

- **音标**: kæm'pein
- **释义**: n. 战役, 运动, 竞选运动\nvi. 参加运动, 作战
- **英文释义**: a series of organized activities designed to achieve a specific goal, especially in politics, marketing, or warfare
- **词根词缀**: camp(田野)+aign(名词)→在田野上作战→战役
- **例句**:
- The environmental group launched a nationwide campaign to reduce plastic waste.
- She campaigned vigorously for the Democratic nomination during the presidential election.
- **考频**: 134
## candidate

- **音标**: 'kændideit
- **释义**: n. 候选人, 投考者\n[法] 候选, 候补者
- **英文释义**: a person who is nominated or applies for a position, office, or honor
- **词根词缀**: candid(白色)+ate(人)→穿白衣的人→候选人
- **例句**:
- She is a strong candidate for the presidency due to her extensive diplomatic experience.
- All candidates must submit their applications and supporting documents by the deadline.
- **考频**: 121
## chancellor

- **音标**: 'tʃɑ:nsәlә
- **释义**: n. 大臣, 总理, 首相, 大使馆/领事馆的一等秘书, 司法官, 大学校长
- **英文释义**: A high-ranking official, such as the head of government in some countries (e.g., Germany), the chief administrative officer of a university, or a senior diplomatic or judicial officer.
- **词根词缀**: chanc(法庭)+ell(or)(人)→法庭官员→大臣
- **例句**:
- The German Chancellor announced new climate policies during the EU summit.
- As Chancellor of Oxford University, she presided over the graduation ceremony and delivered the keynote address.
- **考频**: 14
## coalition

- **音标**: .kәuә'liʃәn
- **释义**: n. 结合体, 结合, 联合\n[经] 联合, 联盟
- **英文释义**: a temporary alliance or partnership of distinct groups, parties, or nations for a common purpose
- **词根词缀**: co(共同)+al(生长)+ition(名词)→共同生长→联盟
- **例句**:
- The two opposition parties formed a coalition to challenge the ruling government in the upcoming election.
- International efforts to address climate change require a broad coalition of governments, NGOs, and private sector actors.
- **考频**: 8
## commonwealth

- **音标**: 'kɔmәnwelθ
- **释义**: n. 共和国；联邦；国民整体
- **英文释义**: a political community founded on law and united by common citizenship and shared interests; often used to denote a republic or a voluntary association of sovereign states
- **词根词缀**: common(共同)+wealth(财富)→共同财富→联邦
- **例句**:
- The Commonwealth of Australia is a federal parliamentary constitutional monarchy.
- The United States was founded as a commonwealth, emphasizing popular sovereignty and the collective welfare of its citizens.
- **考频**: 6
## charter

- **音标**: 'tʃɑ:tә
- **释义**: n. 特许状, 执照, 宪章\nvt. 特许, 发给特许执照
- **英文释义**: A formal document granting rights, privileges, or authority; or the act of officially authorizing or hiring something (e.g., a vehicle) for a specific purpose.
- **词根词缀**: chart(纸)+er(名词)→文件→宪章
- **例句**:
- The Magna Carta is a foundational charter that limited the power of the English monarchy.
- The university was established by royal charter in the 13th century.
- **考频**: 24
