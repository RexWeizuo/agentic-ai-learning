# 主题：商业与经济

> **类型**: 主题

> **学习提示**: 商业与金融场景核心词。auction(拍卖), bankrupt(破产), bargain(交易), broker(经纪人), budget(预算), breach(违约)等。

**10 词**

---

## auction

- **音标**: 'ɒ:kʃәn
- **释义**: ==n. 拍卖\nvt. 拍卖==
- **英文释义**: a public sale where goods or property are sold to the highest bidder
- **同义词**: sale, bidding
- **词根词缀**: auct(增加)+ion→价格不断增加→拍卖
- **例句**:
- The rare manuscript was sold at auction for over $2 million.
- The government decided to auction off surplus land to raise revenue.
- **考频**: 9
## bankrupt

- **音标**: 'bæŋkrʌpt
- **释义**: n. 破产者\na. 破产的\nvt. 使破产
- **英文释义**: legally declared unable to pay one's debts
- **词根词缀**: bank(银行)+rupt(打破)→银行破产→破产
- **例句**:
- The company was declared bankrupt after failing to restructure its massive debt.
- Persistent mismanagement bankrupted the family business within five years.
- **考频**: 13
## bargain

- **音标**: 'bɑ:gin
- **释义**: n. 交易, 买卖协定, 特价商品\nv. 讲价, 交易
- **英文释义**: A formal agreement between parties, especially concerning the sale of goods or services; or an item sold at a reduced price.
- **词根词缀**: barg(争论)+ain(名词)→讨价还价→便宜货
- **例句**:
- The two companies signed a bargain to jointly develop the new technology.
- She bargained fiercely with the vendor and finally bought the antique vase for half the original price.
- **考频**: 41
## broker

- **音标**: 'brәukә
- **释义**: n. 掮客, 经纪人\n[经] 经纪人, 掮客
- **英文释义**: A person or firm that acts as an intermediary between buyers and sellers, especially in financial markets or real estate.
- **词根词缀**: brok(破)+er(人)→打破交易的人→经纪人
- **例句**:
- The stockbroker executed the client's order to buy 500 shares of the tech company.
- Before signing the lease, she hired a real estate broker to negotiate the terms on her behalf.
- **考频**: 13
## budget

- **音标**: 'bʌdʒit
- **释义**: n. 预算\nvi. 编预算\nvt. 编入预算, 安排\na. 廉价的
- **英文释义**: A plan that estimates income and expenses over a specific period, or the act of planning such a plan; also used as an adjective meaning low-cost.
- **词根词缀**: budg(皮包)+et(小)→小皮包→预算
- **例句**:
- The government announced a $2 trillion budget to address climate change and infrastructure renewal.
- Graduate students must carefully budget their limited stipends to cover tuition, housing, and research expenses.
- **考频**: 127
## breach

- **音标**: bri:tʃ
- **释义**: n. 裂口, 违背, 破坏, 违反, 突破, 破裂\nvt. 攻破, 突破\nvi. 跳出水面
- **英文释义**: A violation of a law, agreement, or code of conduct; a break or gap in a structure or system
- **词根词缀**: breach(打破)→打破→违反
- **例句**:
- The company was sued for breach of contract after failing to deliver the goods on time.
- The army launched a surprise assault to breach the enemy's defensive line.
- **考频**: 4
## asset

- **音标**: 'æset
- **释义**: ==n. 资产, 有益的东西==
- **英文释义**: A resource with economic value that an individual, corporation, or country owns or controls with the expectation that it will provide future benefit.
- **同义词**: property, resource
- **反义词**: liability
- **词根词缀**: ass(分配)+et→分配到的财物→资产
- **例句**:
- Human capital is a critical intangible asset for knowledge-based economies.
- The company's most valuable asset is its proprietary algorithm, which gives it a sustainable competitive advantage.
- **考频**: 75
## bill

- **音标**: bil
- **释义**: n. 帐单, 清单, 钞票, 鸟嘴, 广告, 法案, 票据\nvt. 开帐单, (用招贴)宣布
- **英文释义**: A written statement of money owed for goods or services; a piece of paper money; a formal legislative proposal; the hard projecting beak of a bird.
- **词根词缀**: bill(账单)→账单
- **例句**:
- The restaurant manager asked me to settle the bill before leaving.
- The Senate is expected to vote on the climate protection bill next week.
- **考频**: 206
## brand

- **音标**: brænd
- **释义**: n. 商标, 牌子, 烙印\nvt. 打烙印于
- **英文释义**: a distinctive name, symbol, or design that identifies a product or company
- **词根词缀**: brand(燃烧)→烙印→品牌
- **例句**:
- Apple has built a globally recognized brand synonymous with innovation and premium quality.
- The company decided to brand its new eco-friendly line with a minimalist logo to emphasize sustainability.
- **考频**: 126
## business

- **音标**: 'biznis
- **释义**: n. 生意, 事情, 业务, 商业, 商行, 职责\n[经] 企业, 商业, 营业
- **英文释义**: Commercial activity involving the buying and selling of goods or services, or a particular company or organization engaged in such activity.
- **词根词缀**: busy(忙碌)+ness(名词)→忙碌的事→生意
- **例句**:
- Starting a business requires careful planning, sufficient capital, and a clear understanding of market demand.
- It is not her business to decide whether the policy is fair—her role is strictly administrative.
- **考频**: 1128
