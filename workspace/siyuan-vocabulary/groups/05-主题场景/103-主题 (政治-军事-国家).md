# 主题 (政治/军事/国家)

> **类型**: 主题

> **学习提示**: 国家与军事场景。senate(参议院), sovereign(君主/独立的), siege(围攻), salute(敬礼)。

**7 词**

---

## senate

- **音标**: 'senit
- **释义**: n. 参议院, 立法机构, 评议会
- **英文释义**: the upper house of a bicameral legislature, especially in the United States or ancient Rome
- **词根词缀**: sen(老)+ate(名词)→元老院
- **例句**:
- The bill was approved by the Senate after weeks of intense debate.
- She was elected to the U.S. Senate in 2020, becoming the youngest senator in her state's history.
- **考频**: 16
## senator

- **音标**: 'senәtә
- **释义**: n. 参议员, (某些大学的)理事\n[法] 参议员, 上议员
- **英文释义**: a member of the upper house of a legislature, especially the U.S. Senate or a similar body
- **词根词缀**: sen(老)+ator(人)→参议员
- **例句**:
- Senator Smith introduced a bill to reform higher education funding.
- As a tenured professor and university senator, she played a key role in shaping academic policy.
- **考频**: 26
## sovereign

- **音标**: 'sɒvrin
- **释义**: n. 元首, 独立国\na. 具有主权的, 至高无上的, 国王的, 完全的
- **英文释义**: a supreme ruler, especially a monarch; or a state that is independent and self-governing
- **词根词缀**: sover(上)+eign(统治)→最高统治者→君主
- **例句**:
- The United Kingdom is a sovereign state with its own parliament and legal system.
- As the sovereign of the realm, the monarch holds ceremonial authority but exercises no direct political power.
- **考频**: 9
## spokesman

- **音标**: 'spәuksmәn
- **释义**: n. 发言人, 代言者
- **英文释义**: A person who speaks officially for a group, organization, or individual.
- **词根词缀**: spoke(说)+s+man(人)→说话的人→发言人
- **例句**:
- The company's spokesman denied the allegations in a press conference.
- When asked about the policy change, the minister referred reporters to her official spokesman.
- **考频**: 13
## soldier

- **音标**: 'sәuldʒә
- **释义**: n. 军人, 士兵, 兵蚁\nvi. 从军, 尽职, 偷懒, 磨洋工
- **英文释义**: a person who serves in an army, especially a private soldier
- **词根词缀**: sold(薪饷)+ier(人)→拿薪饷的人→士兵
- **例句**:
- As a disciplined soldier, he carried out every order without hesitation.
- She decided to soldier on despite the overwhelming workload and lack of support.
- **考频**: 56
## siege

- **音标**: si:dʒ
- **释义**: n. 包围, 围攻, 再三的努力\nvt. 包围, 围攻
- **英文释义**: a military operation in which forces surround a place to cut off supplies and force surrender
- **词根词缀**: sieg(坐)→围坐→围攻
- **例句**:
- The city endured a six-month siege before finally capitulating to the enemy forces.
- Modern activists launched a sustained siege of public attention through social media campaigns to demand policy reform.
- **考频**: 1
## salute

- **音标**: sә'lu:t
- **释义**: vi. 行礼, 致意, 问候, 放礼炮\nvt. 向...致意, 迎接, 赞扬\nn. 招呼, 敬礼, 礼炮
- **英文释义**: to greet or show respect to someone formally, often with a gesture (e.g., hand to forehead) or words; also, the act or instance of doing so
- **词根词缀**: sal(salt/leap/health)+ute
- **例句**:
- The soldiers saluted their commanding officer as he entered the parade ground.
- In his keynote speech, she saluted the researchers whose groundbreaking work had transformed the field.
