## nature

- **音标**: 'neitʃә
- **释义**: n. 自然, 大自然, 本性, 性格, 性质\n[医] 自然, 大自然; 本性, 性能
- **英文释义**: the physical world and all living things in it, or the inherent qualities or characteristics of a person, thing, or phenomenon
- **词根词缀**: nat(出生)+ure(名词)→天生→自然
- **例句**:
- Human activities have profoundly altered the balance of nature.
- The experimental results suggest that the drug's therapeutic effect is closely linked to its chemical nature.
- **考频**: 350
## mineral

- **音标**: 'minәrәl
- **释义**: n. 矿物, 无机物, 苏打水\na. 矿物的, 似矿物的
- **英文释义**: A naturally occurring inorganic substance with a definite chemical composition and crystalline structure.
- **词根词缀**: miner(矿)+al(名词)→矿物
- **例句**:
- Calcium, iron, and zinc are essential minerals for human health.
- The geologist collected samples of rare minerals from the newly discovered vein.
- **考频**: 30
## molecule

- **音标**: 'mɒlikju:l
- **释义**: n. 分子, 些微\n[化] 分子
- **英文释义**: The smallest unit of a chemical compound that retains its chemical properties, consisting of two or more atoms bonded together.
- **词根词缀**: mole(摩尔)+cule(小)→小摩尔→分子
- **例句**:
- Water is composed of H₂O molecules, each containing two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom.
- Understanding how drug molecules interact with cellular receptors is crucial in pharmaceutical research.
- **考频**: 16
## nitrogen

- **音标**: 'naitrәdʒәn
- **释义**: n. 氮\n[化] 氮N-2
- **英文释义**: A colorless, odorless, gaseous chemical element (symbol N) that makes up about 78% of the Earth's atmosphere and is essential for all living organisms.
- **词根词缀**: nitro(硝基)+gen(产生)→产生硝基→氮气
- **例句**:
- Nitrogen is a key component of amino acids and nucleic acids, making it fundamental to protein synthesis and genetic material.
- In agriculture, excessive use of nitrogen-based fertilizers can lead to water pollution and eutrophication.
- **考频**: 4
## nucleus

- **音标**: 'nju:kliәs
- **释义**: n. 核心, 核子, 原子核, 起点\n[计] 核心程序
- **英文释义**: The central and most important part of something; in physics, the positively charged center of an atom consisting of protons and neutrons.
- **词根词缀**: nucle(核)+us(名词)→核心
- **例句**:
- The nucleus of the cell controls its activities and contains genetic material.
- In modern operating systems, the kernel serves as the nucleus of the system, managing hardware resources and enabling communication between software and hardware.

## metric

- **音标**: 'metrik
- **释义**: a. 公尺的, 公制的, 计量的\n[医] 测量的, 米制的, 公制的
- **英文释义**: Relating to the metric system or to measurement, especially in science, medicine, or data analysis.
- **词根词缀**: metr(测量)+ic(形容词)→公制的

## metre

- **音标**: 'mi:tә
- **释义**: n. 公尺, 格律, 韵律\n[医] 米, 公尺
- **英文释义**: The basic unit of length in the International System of Units (SI), equal to approximately 39.37 inches; also, the rhythmic structure of a verse line in poetry.
- **词根词缀**: metre(测量)→米
- **例句**:
- The experiment required precise measurements in metres to ensure data consistency.
- Shakespeare's iambic pentameter demonstrates a strict metrical pattern central to English poetic tradition.
- **考频**: 19
## millimetre

- **音标**: 'milimi:tә
- **释义**: n. 毫米\n[机] 公厘
- **英文释义**: A unit of length equal to one thousandth of a metre.
- **词根词缀**: milli(千分之一/毫)+metre
- **例句**:
- The precision required for this aerospace component is within 0.1 millimetre.
- In the lab report, all measurements were recorded in millimetres to ensure consistency with SI units.
