# f- 命运/失败/缺陷

> **类型**: 主题

> **学习提示**: 与失败、命运、缺陷相关的负面词汇

**5 词**

---

## fail

- **音标**: feil
- **释义**: vi. 失败, 缺乏, 中断, 衰退, 失灵\nvt. 忘记, 使...失望, 缺乏, 不及格\nn. 不及格
- **英文释义**: to not succeed in doing something; to be unable to fulfill an expectation, requirement, or function
- **词根词缀**: fail(失败)→失败
- **例句**:
- The experiment failed to produce the expected results, prompting a redesign of the methodology.
- She failed her qualifying exam, but after intensive preparation, she passed on the second attempt.
- **考频**: 363
## failure

- **音标**: 'feiljә
- **释义**: n. 失败, 失败者, 不足, 缺乏, 破产\n[计] 故障; 失效
- **英文释义**: the lack of success in achieving a desired outcome; an instance of something not working as intended
- **词根词缀**: fail(失败)+ure(名词)→失败
- **例句**:
- His repeated business ventures ended in failure, culminating in bankruptcy.
- The system's sudden failure during the critical experiment highlighted serious design flaws.
- **考频**: 226
## fatal

- **音标**: 'feitәl
- **释义**: a. 致命的, 重大的, 命运注定的, 粗心的, 不幸的\n[医] 致命的, 致死的
- **英文释义**: causing death or failure; extremely serious or decisive
- **词根词缀**: fat(命运)+al(形容词)→命运的→致命的
- **例句**:
- The accident was caused by a fatal error in judgment.
- A single fatal flaw in the argument undermined the entire thesis.
- **考频**: 49
## fate

- **音标**: feit
- **释义**: n. 命运, 运气\nvt. 注定
- **英文释义**: the development of events beyond a person's control, regarded as predetermined by a supernatural power or inevitable circumstance
- **词根词缀**: fat(命运)+e→命运
- **例句**:
- Her fate was sealed the moment she decided to defy the company's ethical code.
- The treaty was widely seen as a turning point that would determine the fate of the entire region.
- **考频**: 32
## faulty

- **音标**: 'fɒ:lti
- **释义**: a. 有过失的, 有缺点的, 不完美的, 应受责备的\n[经] 缺点多的
- **英文释义**: containing defects or errors; not working or made properly
- **词根词缀**: fault(错误)+y(形容词)→有错误的
- **例句**:
- The product was recalled due to a faulty design that posed safety risks.
- Her argument collapsed under scrutiny because of several faulty assumptions.
- **考频**: 21
