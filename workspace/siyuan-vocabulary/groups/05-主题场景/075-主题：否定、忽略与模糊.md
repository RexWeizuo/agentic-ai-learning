# 主题：否定、忽略与模糊

> **类型**: 主题

> **学习提示**: 表示否定、忽视、遗漏或模糊不清的词汇。

**5 词**

---

## negative

- **音标**: 'negәtiv
- **释义**: n. 否定, 否定语, 负数, 底片\na. 否定的, 消极的, 负的, 阴性的\nn. 负数, 负值\n[计] 负数, 负值
- **英文释义**: expressing denial, refusal, or opposition; having a value less than zero; indicating pessimism or lack of support
- **词根词缀**: neg(否定)+ative(形容词)→否定的
- **例句**:
- The committee gave a negative response to the proposal, citing insufficient evidence.
- In mathematics, subtracting a larger number from a smaller one yields a negative result.
- **考频**: 201
## neglect

- **音标**: ni'glekt
- **释义**: n. 疏忽, 忽略, 漏做\nvt. 疏忽, 忽视, 不顾
- **英文释义**: failure to give proper attention or care to something; to ignore or fail to do something important
- **词根词缀**: neg(不)+lect(选择)→不选择→忽视
- **例句**:
- The child's poor health was attributed to parental neglect.
- Candidates who neglect to review past exam papers often struggle with time management in the actual test.
- **考频**: 82
## negligible

- **音标**: 'neglidʒәbl
- **释义**: a. 可以忽略的, 不足取的, 无用的\n[医] 可不计的, 可忽视的
- **英文释义**: so small or unimportant that it can be ignored
- **词根词缀**: neg(不)+lig(选择)+ible(能)→能不被选择的→可忽略的
- **例句**:
- The risk of side effects from this drug is negligible.
- Any improvement in fuel efficiency at such high speeds is negligible.
- **考频**: 9
## omit

- **音标**: әu'mit
- **释义**: vt. 省略, 删除, 疏忽, 遗漏\n[化] 省略
- **英文释义**: to leave something out, either intentionally or accidentally
- **词根词缀**: o(离开)+mit(送)→送走→省略
- **例句**:
- The editor omitted several redundant paragraphs to improve the article's clarity.
- She omitted mentioning her prior experience in the interview, which may have weakened her application.
- **考频**: 11
## obscure

- **音标**: әb'skjuә
- **释义**: a. 模糊的, 昏暗的, 难解的, 晦涩的, 偏僻的, 微贱的, 无名的\nvt. 使变暗, 隐藏, 使变模糊, 使难解
- **英文释义**: not clear, easy to understand, or see; not famous or important
- **词根词缀**: ob(加强)+scur(覆盖)+e→完全覆盖→模糊的
- **例句**:
- The author's argument is deliberately obscure, making it difficult for readers to grasp the central thesis.
- Her early poems remained obscure until a major literary journal republished them decades later.
- **考频**: 44
