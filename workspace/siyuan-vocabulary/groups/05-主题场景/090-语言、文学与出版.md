# 语言、文学与出版

> **类型**: 主题

> **学习提示**: 涵盖文学体裁(prose)、语法(pronoun)、修辞(rhetoric)及出版宣传(publicity/propaganda)。

**7 词**

---

## propaganda

- **音标**: .prɒpә'gændә
- **释义**: n. 宣传, 宣传活动\n[医] 宣传
- **英文释义**: Information, especially biased or misleading, used to promote a particular political cause or point of view.
- **词根词缀**: pro(向前)+pag(传播)+anda(名词)→传播→宣传
- **例句**:
- The government's campaign was widely criticized as blatant propaganda designed to manipulate public opinion.
- Historians warn that wartime propaganda often distorts facts to foster national unity and demonize the enemy.
- **考频**: 5
## pronoun

- **音标**: 'prәunaun
- **释义**: n. 代词
- **英文释义**: A word that substitutes for a noun or noun phrase, such as 'he', 'she', 'it', 'they', or 'this'.
- **词根词缀**: pro(代替)+noun(名词)→代替名词→代词
- **例句**:
- The pronoun 'they' is increasingly used as a singular, gender-neutral form in academic writing.
- In the sentence 'She gave it to him', 'she', 'it', and 'him' are all pronouns functioning as subject, object, and indirect object respectively.
- **考频**: 14
## prose

- **音标**: prәuz
- **释义**: n. 散文\nv. 写散文\na. 散文的, 平凡的
- **英文释义**: written or spoken language in its ordinary form, without metrical structure or rhyme; contrasted with poetry
- **词根词缀**: pro(向前)+se(跟随)→散文
- **例句**:
- Her novel blends poetic imagery with lucid prose.
- The essay is praised for its clarity and precision of prose.
- **考频**: 11
## rhetoric

- **音标**: 'retәrik
- **释义**: n. 修辞, 修辞学, 雄辩(术)
- **英文释义**: The art of effective or persuasive speaking or writing, especially the use of figures of speech and other compositional techniques.
- **词根词缀**: rhetor(演说家)+ic(形容词)→演说的→修辞
- **例句**:
- The politician's speech was full of empty rhetoric rather than concrete policy proposals.
- In academic writing, excessive rhetorical flourish can undermine clarity and credibility.
- **考频**: 4
## publication

- **音标**: .pʌbli'keiʃәn
- **释义**: n. 出版物, 出版, 公布\n[经] 发布
- **英文释义**: a printed or digital work issued for public distribution, such as a book, journal, or report; the act of making information publicly available
- **词根词缀**: public(公共)+ation(名词)→使公开→出版
- **例句**:
- The journal's latest publication features groundbreaking research on climate change adaptation.
- Before the official publication of the economic data, analysts had already speculated about the figures based on preliminary indicators.
- **考频**: 76
## publicity

- **音标**: pʌb'lisiti
- **释义**: n. 名声, 宣传, 公开场合\n[经] 宣传, 广告
- **英文释义**: the activity of attracting public attention to a product, person, or organization, often through the media
- **词根词缀**: public(公共)+ity(名词)→公开→宣传
- **例句**:
- The celebrity's controversial remarks generated massive publicity, boosting album sales despite the backlash.
- While the startup sought positive publicity through social media campaigns, it struggled to convert attention into sustainable customer loyalty.
- **考频**: 25
## publish

- **音标**: 'pʌbliʃ
- **释义**: vt. 出版, 发行, 公开, 发表, 宣传, 公布\nvi. 出版, 发行
- **英文释义**: to make information, a book, article, or other material available to the public, especially in print or online
- **词根词缀**: publ(公共)+ish(动词)→使公开→出版
- **例句**:
- The academic journal will publish her groundbreaking research next month.
- Before publishing sensitive data, the company must obtain explicit consent from all participants.
- **考频**: 238
