# 犯罪/侵入/违法

> **类型**: 主题场景

> **学习提示**: 法律与冲突场景核心词。hijack (劫持)；hostage (人质)。

**5 词**

---

## hijack

- **音标**: 'haidʒæk
- **释义**: vt. 抢劫, 劫持, 敲诈\n[法] 挡路抢劫, 抢劫, 绑架
- **英文释义**: to seize control of a vehicle, especially an aircraft, by force in order to divert it to a different destination; also used figuratively to take over or divert something (e.g., a discussion, process, or system) for one's own purposes
- **词根词缀**: hijack(劫持)→劫持
- **例句**:
- Armed men hijacked the passenger plane and forced it to land in a neighboring country.
- The meeting was hijacked by a single participant who dominated the discussion with unrelated political rhetoric.
- **考频**: 14
## hostage

- **音标**: 'hɒstidʒ
- **释义**: n. 人质, 抵押品\n[经] 人质, 抵押品
- **英文释义**: a person seized or held as security for the fulfillment of a condition, especially by force or threat
- **词根词缀**: host(主人)+age(名词)→被主人扣留的人→人质
- **例句**:
- The terrorists demanded the release of their comrades in exchange for the hostages.
- Diplomatic negotiations intensified after three foreign journalists were taken hostage in the conflict zone.
- **考频**: 4
## illegal

- **音标**: i'li:gәl
- **释义**: a. 违法的, 不合规定的\n[经] 非法的, 犯规的
- **英文释义**: not allowed by law or rules
- **词根词缀**: il(不)+leg(法律)+al→不合法的
- **例句**:
- The company was fined for engaging in illegal dumping of hazardous waste.
- Possession of such substances is illegal in most countries, and violations carry severe penalties.
- **考频**: 61
## invade

- **音标**: in'veid
- **释义**: vt. 侵入, 拥入, 侵略, 侵袭\n[法] 强入, 侵犯, 侵略
- **英文释义**: to enter a place or area in large numbers, often causing harm or disruption; to attack and take control of a country or region by military force
- **词根词缀**: in(进入)+vad(走)+e→走入→入侵
- **例句**:
- The army launched a surprise offensive to invade the neighboring country's territory.
- Social media platforms are increasingly invaded by algorithm-driven advertisements that disrupt user experience.
- **考频**: 21
## invasion

- **音标**: in'veiʒәn
- **释义**: n. 侵犯, 侵入, 侵害\n[医] 侵袭, 侵入, 发病
- **英文释义**: the act of entering a place or country with armed forces in order to take control, or the act of entering somewhere in a way that is unwelcome or harmful
- **词根词缀**: in(进入)+vas(走)+ion(名词)→入侵
- **例句**:
- The historian argued that the Norman invasion of England in 1066 fundamentally transformed its language and governance.
- In oncology, tumor invasion refers to the spread of malignant cells into surrounding healthy tissues, a key indicator of cancer progression.
- **考频**: 10
