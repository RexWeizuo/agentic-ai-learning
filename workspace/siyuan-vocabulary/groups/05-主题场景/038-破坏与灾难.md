# 破坏与灾难

> **类型**: 主题

> **学习提示**: 描述负面事件、系统性破坏及由此引发的心理绝望状态。

**8 词**

---

## destruction

- **音标**: di'strʌkʃәn
- **释义**: n. 破坏, 毁灭\n[医] 破坏
- **英文释义**: the act or process of destroying something or the state of being destroyed
- **词根词缀**: de(去掉)+struct(建造)+ion(名词)→破坏
- **例句**:
- The earthquake caused widespread destruction to infrastructure and housing.
- Environmental scientists warn that unchecked deforestation may lead to irreversible ecological destruction.
- **考频**: 56
## destructive

- **音标**: di'strʌktiv
- **释义**: a. 毁灭性的, 破坏的, 有害的\n[医] 破坏的, 毁坏的
- **英文释义**: causing great damage or harm
- **词根词缀**: de(去掉)+struct(建造)+ive(形容词)→破坏性的
- **例句**:
- The hurricane had a destructive impact on coastal infrastructure.
- Chronic stress can have a destructive effect on both physical and mental health.
- **考频**: 29
## disaster

- **音标**: di'zɑ:stә
- **释义**: n. 灾祸, 不幸, 彻底失败
- **英文释义**: a sudden event causing great damage, suffering, or loss
- **词根词缀**: dis(否定)+aster(星)→星位不正→灾难
- **例句**:
- The earthquake was a natural disaster that left thousands homeless.
- Her presentation was such a disaster that she was asked to revise the entire proposal before resubmission.
- **考频**: 122
## disastrous

- **音标**: di'zɑ:strәs
- **释义**: a. 损失惨重的, 悲伤的
- **英文释义**: causing great harm, damage, or suffering; extremely unsuccessful
- **词根词缀**: dis(否定)+aster(星)+ous(形容词)→星位不正的→灾难性的
- **例句**:
- The company's disastrous financial decisions led to its bankruptcy within a year.
- Her disastrous attempt to negotiate the treaty alienated all key allies.
- **考频**: 19
## disrupt

- **音标**: dis'rʌpt
- **释义**: a. 分裂的, 中断的\nvt. 使分裂, 使瓦解
- **英文释义**: to interrupt or cause disorder in a process, system, or activity
- **词根词缀**: dis(分开)+rupt(打破)→打破→中断
- **例句**:
- The sudden power outage disrupted the online exam, forcing the university to reschedule it.
- Technological innovations continue to disrupt traditional industries such as publishing and retail.
- **考频**: 15
## disorder

- **音标**: dis'ɒ:dә
- **释义**: n. 杂乱, 混乱\nvt. 扰乱, 使失调
- **英文释义**: A lack of order or regular arrangement; a disturbance or disruption of normal function or structure
- **词根词缀**: dis(否定)+order(顺序)→没有顺序→混乱
- **例句**:
- The sudden power outage caused complete disorder in the hospital's emergency department.
- Chronic stress can disorder the body's hormonal balance, leading to serious health issues.
- **考频**: 54
## distress

- **音标**: di'stres
- **释义**: n. 苦恼, 贫困, 痛苦\nvt. 使苦恼\na. 亏本出售的
- **英文释义**: a state of extreme anxiety, sorrow, or pain; to cause such suffering
- **词根词缀**: di(加强)+stress(压)→压紧→痛苦
- **例句**:
- The sudden death of her father threw her into deep distress.
- The company was forced to sell its assets at a distress price to meet urgent debt obligations.
- **考频**: 24
## despair

- **音标**: di'spєә
- **释义**: n. 绝望, 失望\nvi. 绝望
- **英文释义**: the complete loss or absence of hope
- **词根词缀**: de(去掉)+spair(希望)→失去希望→绝望
