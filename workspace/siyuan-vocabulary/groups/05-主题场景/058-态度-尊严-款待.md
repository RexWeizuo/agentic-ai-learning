# 态度/尊严/款待

> **类型**: 主题场景

> **学习提示**: hospitality 指好客、款待；humiliate 指使蒙羞、屈辱，与 humble (谦逊的) 词根同源但感情色彩不同。

**6 词**

---

## hostile

- **音标**: 'hɒstail
- **释义**: a. 敌人的, 怀敌意的, 敌对的\nn. 敌对分子
- **英文释义**: unfriendly, antagonistic, or opposed to someone or something
- **词根词缀**: host(敌人)+ile(形容词)→敌人的→敌对的
- **例句**:
- The company faced hostile takeovers from several rival firms.
- Her hostile attitude during the negotiation made it difficult to reach a compromise.
- **考频**: 35
## honor

- **音标**: 'ɒnә
- **释义**: n. 荣誉, 头衔, 信用, 尊敬, 名誉, 阁下, 勋章\nvt. 尊敬, 授予荣誉, 承兑, 实践
- **英文释义**: high respect, esteem, or recognition for admirable qualities or achievements; also, to show respect or fulfill a commitment
- **词根词缀**: honor(荣誉)→荣誉
- **例句**:
- She received the Nobel Prize in Physics as the highest honor for her groundbreaking research.
- The university will honor its distinguished alumni at the commencement ceremony by conferring honorary doctorates.
- **考频**: 55
## honorable

- **音标**: 'ɒnәrәbl
- **释义**: a. 值得尊敬的, 荣耀的, 高贵的
- **英文释义**: deserving respect and admiration because of high moral standards or honorable conduct
- **词根词缀**: honor(荣誉)+able(能…的)→能获得荣誉的→可敬的
- **例句**:
- Her honorable service to the community earned her national recognition.
- It is not honorable to achieve success through deception, even if it goes undetected.
- **考频**: 4
## hospitality

- **音标**: .hɒspi'tæliti
- **释义**: n. 款待, 亲切, 殷勤
- **英文释义**: the friendly and generous reception and entertainment of guests, visitors, or strangers
- **词根词缀**: hospit(客人)+ality(名词)→好客
- **例句**:
- The hotel is renowned for its exceptional hospitality and attention to detail.
- Her warmth and natural hospitality made all the international delegates feel welcome at the conference.
- **考频**: 20
## humble

- **音标**: 'hʌmbl
- **释义**: a. 低下的, 谦逊的, 粗陋的\nvt. 使...卑下, 降低...地位, 贬抑
- **英文释义**: having a modest or low view of one's importance; not proud or arrogant
- **词根词缀**: hum(地面)+ble(形容词)→接近地面的→谦卑的
- **例句**:
- Despite his remarkable achievements, he remains humble and approachable.
- The criticism humbled her, prompting a sincere reassessment of her leadership style.
- **考频**: 19
## humiliate

- **音标**: hju:'milieit
- **释义**: vt. 使丢脸, 使蒙羞, 屈辱
- **英文释义**: to make someone feel ashamed and foolish by injuring their dignity or self-respect
- **词根词缀**: hum(地面)+iliate(使)→使降到地面→羞辱
- **例句**:
- Publicly criticizing a student in front of the class can seriously humiliate them and damage their confidence.
- The candidate was deeply humiliated when his fabricated credentials were exposed during the televised debate.
- **考频**: 3
