# em- 帝国/显赫

> **类型**: 主题

> **学习提示**: 帝国、使馆与显赫地位相关词汇

**4 词**

---

## emperor

- **音标**: 'empәrә
- **释义**: n. 皇帝, 君主
- **英文释义**: a male sovereign ruler of an empire, especially one who rules by hereditary right or supreme authority
- **词根词缀**: em(加强)+per(命令)+or(人)→命令者→皇帝
- **例句**:
- The Roman emperor Augustus established the Pax Romana, a period of relative peace and stability across the empire.
- In East Asian history, the emperor of China was regarded as the Son of Heaven, wielding both political and spiritual authority.
- **考频**: 7
## empire

- **音标**: 'empaiә
- **释义**: n. 帝国, 帝权
- **英文释义**: a large political unit or state ruled by a single authority, especially an emperor or empress
- **词根词缀**: em(加强)+pire(统治)→加强统治→帝国
- **例句**:
- The Roman Empire spanned three continents at its height.
- Modern multinational corporations are sometimes described as economic empires.
- **考频**: 20
## embassy

- **音标**: 'embәsi
- **释义**: n. 大使馆, 大使馆全体人员\n[经] 大使馆
- **英文释义**: The official residence or offices of an ambassador in a foreign country, or the diplomatic mission itself.
- **词根词缀**: em(使/在…内)+bas(base/low)+sy
- **例句**:
- The U.S. embassy in Beijing issued a travel advisory for American citizens.
- She works as a cultural attaché at the British embassy in Tokyo.
## eminent

- **音标**: 'eminәnt
- **释义**: a. 显赫的, 杰出的, 有名的
- **英文释义**: famous and respected within a particular field or society
- **词根词缀**: e(出)+min(突出)+ent(形容词)→突出的→杰出的
- **例句**:
- She is an eminent scholar in the field of cognitive linguistics.
- The conference was attended by several eminent scientists, including two Nobel laureates.
- **考频**: 4
