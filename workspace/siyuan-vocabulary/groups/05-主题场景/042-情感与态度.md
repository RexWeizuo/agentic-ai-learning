# 情感与态度

> **类型**: 主题

> **学习提示**: 表达强烈的主观情绪，包括渴望、厌恶、轻视及恐惧。

**7 词**

---

## desire

- **音标**: di'zaiә
- **释义**: n. 欲望, 要求\nvt. 想要, 请求\nvi. 渴望
- **英文释义**: a strong feeling of wanting to have something or wishing for something to happen
- **词根词缀**: de(加强)+sire(星星)→渴望
- **例句**:
- Her desire for academic excellence drove her to study abroad.
- The committee does not desire further delays in implementing the policy.
- **考频**: 216
## desirable

- **音标**: di'zairәbl
- **释义**: a. 令人想望的, 可取的
- **英文释义**: worth having or wanting; attractive or beneficial
- **词根词缀**: de(加强)+sire(渴望)+able(可...的)→值得渴望的
- **例句**:
- A stable economy is a highly desirable goal for any developing country.
- Critical thinking skills are increasingly desirable in today's competitive job market.
- **考频**: 45
## despise

- **音标**: di'spaiz
- **释义**: vt. 轻视
- **英文释义**: to feel contempt or a deep dislike for someone or something
- **词根词缀**: de(向下)+spise(看)→向下看→鄙视
- **例句**:
- She despises hypocrisy and always speaks her mind.
- Many environmental activists despise corporations that prioritize profit over ecological sustainability.
- **考频**: 7
## disgust

- **音标**: dis'gʌst
- **释义**: n. 厌恶, 嫌恶\nvi. 令人厌恶\nvt. 使作呕
- **英文释义**: a strong feeling of dislike and disapproval, often accompanied by nausea
- **词根词缀**: dis(否定)+gust(味道)→不好的味道→厌恶
- **例句**:
- The graphic violence in the film filled her with disgust.
- His hypocrisy disgusted many voters and contributed to his electoral defeat.
- **考频**: 8
## dislike

- **音标**: dis'laik
- **释义**: n. 嫌恶\nvt. 讨厌, 不喜欢
- **英文释义**: a feeling of not liking someone or something; to feel annoyance or aversion toward
- **词根词缀**: dis(否定)+like(喜欢)→不喜欢
- **例句**:
- Her dislike of authoritarian leadership styles became evident during the team discussion.
- Many students dislike rote memorization, preferring instead to engage in critical analysis of complex texts.
- **考频**: 39
## dismay

- **音标**: dis'mei
- **释义**: n. 沮丧\nvt. 使惊愕, 使气馁
- **英文释义**: a feeling of anxiety, fear, or loss of confidence; to fill someone with such feelings
- **词根词缀**: dis(否定)+may(可能)→失去可能→沮丧
- **例句**:
- The sudden cancellation of the project filled the team with dismay.
- She was dismayed to learn that her research proposal had been rejected without review.
- **考频**: 5
## dread

- **音标**: dred
- **释义**: n. 恐惧, 可怕的人(或事)\na. 可怕的, 可怖的\nv. 恐惧, 害怕
- **英文释义**: a feeling of great fear or anxiety about something that is going to happen
- **词根词缀**: dread→恐惧
- **例句**:
- She felt a deep dread as she walked into the interview room.
- Many students dread the final exam period because of its intensity and high stakes.
- **考频**: 7
