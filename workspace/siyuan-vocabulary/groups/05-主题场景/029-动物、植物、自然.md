## crab

- **音标**: kræb
- **释义**: n. 螃蟹, 偏航, 脾气乖戾的人, 沙果\nvi. 捕蟹, 偏航, 发牢骚\nvt. 使偏航, 抱怨, 破坏
- **英文释义**: A marine crustacean with a broad flattened body and five pairs of legs; also used to describe moving sideways, complaining irritably, or deviating from course.
- **词根词缀**: crab(螃蟹)→螃蟹
- **例句**:
- The crab scuttled sideways across the rocky shore.
- She crabs constantly about minor inconveniences, making team collaboration difficult.
- **考频**: 5
## crane

- **音标**: krein
- **释义**: n. 鹤, 起重机\nvt. 伸颈, 起重机起吊\nvi. 伸颈看, 迟疑
- **英文释义**: 1. A large wading bird with long legs and neck; 2. A machine for lifting and moving heavy objects; 3. To stretch one's neck to see something better or out of curiosity
- **词根词缀**: cran(鹤)→鹤
- **例句**:
- The construction site was equipped with a towering crane to lift steel beams into place.
- She craned her neck to catch a glimpse of the celebrity walking across the plaza.
- **考频**: 5
## cricket

- **音标**: 'krikit
- **释义**: n. 蟋蟀, 板球
- **英文释义**: 1. A small jumping insect with long hind legs and chirping sound produced by rubbing wings; 2. A bat-and-ball team sport played between two teams of eleven players on a field with a wicket at each end.
- **词根词缀**: crick(吱吱叫)+et(小)→吱吱叫的小虫→蟋蟀
- **例句**:
- The chirping of crickets filled the quiet summer night.
- Cricket is immensely popular in India, England, and Australia, requiring exceptional skill in batting, bowling, and fielding.
- **考频**: 6
## crow

- **音标**: krәu
- **释义**: n. 啼叫, 乌鸦, 鸡鸣, 撬棍\nvi. 啼叫, 报晓
- **英文释义**: A large black bird known for its harsh call; also used to refer to the act of making a loud, boastful claim or the crowing sound made by a rooster.
- **词根词缀**: crow(拟声词根)→乌鸦叫声→乌鸦
- **例句**:
- The crow perched on the bare branch, its sharp caw echoing across the silent winter field.
- After winning the national debate championship, he began to crow about his intellectual superiority—though his humility quickly won back his peers.
- **考频**: 2
## cucumber

- **音标**: 'kju:kәmbә
- **释义**: n. 黄瓜\n[医] 黄瓜, 胡瓜
- **英文释义**: A long, green, cylindrical fruit with a mild flavor, commonly eaten raw in salads or pickled.
- **词根词缀**: cucum(黄瓜)+ber→黄瓜
- **例句**:
- She added sliced cucumber and cherry tomatoes to her Greek salad.
- The study found that cucumber contains high water content and low calories, making it ideal for weight-management diets.
- **考频**: 10
