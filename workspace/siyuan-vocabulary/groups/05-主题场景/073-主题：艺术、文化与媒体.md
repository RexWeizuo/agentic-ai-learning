## metaphor

- **音标**: 'metәfә
- **释义**: n. 隐喻
- **英文释义**: A figure of speech in which a word or phrase is applied to something to which it is not literally applicable, to suggest a resemblance.
- **词根词缀**: meta(变化)+phor(携带)→隐喻
- **例句**:
- Time is often described as a river—a powerful metaphor for its continuous, unstoppable flow.
- Her silence was a metaphor for her deep grief, speaking louder than words ever could.
- **考频**: 7
## music

- **音标**: 'mju:zik
- **释义**: n. 音乐, 乐曲
- **英文释义**: artistic expression through organized sound, especially melody, harmony, and rhythm
- **词根词缀**: mus(缪斯)+ic(名词)→缪斯的艺术→音乐
- **例句**:
- Classical music demands both technical precision and deep emotional interpretation.
- The film's evocative music played a crucial role in shaping the audience's psychological response.
- **考频**: 361
## musician

- **音标**: mju:'ziʃәn
- **释义**: n. 音乐家, 乐师, 作曲家
- **英文释义**: a person who plays a musical instrument, especially as a profession
- **词根词缀**: music(音乐)+ian(人)→音乐家
- **例句**:
- As a highly accomplished musician, she has performed with major orchestras across Europe.
- The composer was not only a gifted musician but also a rigorous music theorist, which informed his innovative symphonic structures.
- **考频**: 39
## narrative

- **音标**: 'nærәtiv
- **释义**: n. 叙述, 故事\na. 叙述的, 叙事的, 故事体的
- **英文释义**: A spoken or written account of connected events; a story or the telling of a story.
- **词根词缀**: narr(讲述)+ative(形容词)→叙述的
- **例句**:
- The novel employs a non-linear narrative to heighten suspense and reflect the protagonist's fragmented memory.
- Historians increasingly recognize that dominant political narratives often marginalize minority voices.
- **考频**: 39
## novelty

- **音标**: 'nɒvәlti
- **释义**: n. 新奇, 新鲜, 新奇的事物
- **英文释义**: the quality of being new, original, or unusual; something new or unusual that is often interesting but not usually lasting
- **词根词缀**: nov(新)+el(小)+ty(名词)→新奇
- **例句**:
- The novelty of working from home wore off after a few weeks, and she missed the office environment.
- Tourists were drawn to the festival less by its cultural significance than by the novelty of its traditional costumes.
- **考频**: 8
## mosaic

- **音标**: mәu'zeiik
- **释义**: n. 马赛克, 镶嵌细工, 镶嵌工艺\na. 马赛克的, 镶嵌细工的, 拼花的\nvt. 用镶嵌细工装饰\n[计] Mosaic浏览程序
- **英文释义**: A picture or pattern made by arranging small colored pieces of stone, glass, or tile; also used metaphorically to describe a diverse mixture.
- **词根词缀**: mosaic(马赛克)→马赛克
- **例句**:
- The ancient Roman villa featured a stunning mosaic depicting Neptune and his chariot.
- Modern urban society is often described as a cultural mosaic, reflecting the coexistence of multiple ethnicities, languages, and traditions.
- **考频**: 1
