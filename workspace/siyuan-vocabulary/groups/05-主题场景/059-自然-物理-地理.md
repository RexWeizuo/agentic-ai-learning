## hydrogen

- **音标**: 'haidrәdʒәn
- **释义**: n. 氢\n[化] 氢H-2
- **英文释义**: A colorless, odorless, flammable gas and the lightest and most abundant chemical element in the universe, with atomic number 1.
- **词根词缀**: hydro(水)+gen(产生)→产生水的物质→氢
- **例句**:
- Hydrogen is increasingly used as a clean energy source in fuel cells for electric vehicles.
- In water electrolysis, electricity splits water molecules into oxygen and hydrogen.
- **考频**: 13
## horsepower

- **音标**: 'hɒ:spauә
- **释义**: n. 马力\n[电] 马力
- **英文释义**: A unit of power equal to 746 watts, traditionally defined as the power needed to lift 33,000 pounds one foot in one minute.
- **词根词缀**: horsepow+-er(…的人/…物/更…)
- **例句**:
- The new electric sedan delivers over 400 horsepower, enabling rapid acceleration from 0 to 60 mph in under 4 seconds.
- While horsepower remains a common metric for engine output, many engineers now prefer kilowatts for precision and international standardization.
## horizon

- **音标**: hә'raizәn
- **释义**: n. 地平线, 眼界, (天)视地平
- **英文释义**: the line at which the earth's surface and the sky appear to meet; also used metaphorically to refer to the limit of one's knowledge, experience, or interest
- **词根词缀**: horizon(地平线)→地平线
- **例句**:
- The sun disappeared below the horizon as dusk settled over the coastal town.
- Pursuing interdisciplinary research has significantly broadened her intellectual horizon.
- **考频**: 35
## horizontal

- **音标**: .hɒri'zɒntәl
- **释义**: n. 水平线, 水平面, 水平位置\na. 水平线的, 平坦的, 横的\nn. 水平\n[计] 水平
- **英文释义**: parallel to the horizon; flat and level
- **词根词缀**: horizon(地平线)+tal(形容词)→地平线的→水平的
- **例句**:
- The architect designed the building with long horizontal lines to emphasize stability and balance.
- In data visualization, the x-axis typically represents the horizontal dimension, while the y-axis is vertical.
- **考频**: 4
